# Emotional Intelligence

EQ is the strongest predictor of performance. 58% of career success comes from emotional intelligence.

## The Four Pillars

### 1. Self-Awareness
Recognizing your own emotions and their effects.

**Ask yourself:**
- What am I feeling right now?
- What triggered this emotion?
- How is this affecting my thinking?
- What are my blind spots?

**Reality check:** Only 10-15% of people are truly self-aware. Assume you have blind spots.

### 2. Self-Management
Controlling impulses and adapting to circumstances.

**The Pause Protocol:**
1. Notice the trigger
2. PAUSE before reacting (even 2 seconds helps)
3. Name the emotion ("I'm feeling frustrated")
4. Choose the response (don't react automatically)

**Managing difficult emotions:**
- Frustration → Step back, breathe, reframe
- Anxiety → Focus on what you can control
- Anger → Delay response, channel into action
- Fear → Break into small steps

### 3. Social Awareness
Reading the room, understanding others.

**Detection signals:**
| Signal | Possible Meaning |
|--------|------------------|
| Crossed arms | Defensive or cold |
| Avoiding eye contact | Discomfort or deception |
| Short responses | Disengagement or stress |
| Tone shift | Emotional change |
| Topic avoidance | Sensitive area |

**Empathy modes:**
- **Cognitive**: Understanding their perspective intellectually
- **Emotional**: Feeling what they feel
- **Compassionate**: Understanding + feeling + wanting to help

### 4. Relationship Management
Influencing, developing, and connecting with others.

**Core skills:**
- Inspirational communication
- Conflict resolution
- Coaching and developing others
- Building trust through consistency

## High-EQ Responses

**When someone is frustrated:**
- ❌ "Calm down" (invalidating)
- ❌ "It's not a big deal" (dismissive)
- ✅ "That sounds incredibly difficult"
- ✅ "I can see why you'd feel that way"

**When giving difficult feedback:**
- ❌ "You always do X wrong"
- ✅ "I noticed X happened. What's your perspective?"
- ✅ "Here's the impact I observed..."

**When someone is anxious:**
- ❌ "Don't worry about it"
- ✅ "What's the worst case? Let's think through it together"
- ✅ "What's the one thing you can control right now?"

## The EQ Decision Framework

Before major decisions, ask:
1. What am I feeling about this?
2. How are emotions influencing my judgment?
3. What would I decide if I felt neutral?
4. How will others feel about this decision?
5. What's the right thing to do regardless of feelings?

## NEVER Do

- ❌ Dismiss emotions as "irrational"
- ❌ React immediately when triggered
- ❌ Assume your perception is objective
- ❌ Ignore emotional undercurrents in conversations
- ❌ Use "you always" or "you never" statements
- ❌ Fix problems before acknowledging feelings

## The Validation Sequence

When someone shares something difficult:

1. **Acknowledge**: "Thank you for sharing that"
2. **Validate**: "That makes complete sense"
3. **Normalize**: "Many people experience this"
4. **Support**: "What would feel most helpful right now?"

Don't jump to solutions. Sometimes people need to be heard, not fixed.
