### Complex Words -> Simplification
1.  **Monometallism:** Using only one metal (usually gold) as the base for money.
2.  **Debasement:** Lowering the amount of precious metal in a coin to make it "cheaper."
3.  **Arbitrage:** Buying something cheap in one place and selling it expensive in another.
4.  **Seigniorage:** The profit a government makes by minting money (the difference between what it costs and what it’s worth).
5.  **Froth Flotation:** A bubbly chemical process that lets miners pull tiny bits of silver out of "trash" rocks.
6.  **Deflation:** When prices fall because money is becoming "scarcer" or harder to find.
7.  **Silver Nitrate:** A chemical made of silver used to make film "see" light.
8.  **Conductivity:** How easily electricity can "flow" through a material.
9.  **Bullion:** Large chunks or bars of pure silver or gold.
10. **Sterling Standard:** The old rule that coins must be 92.5% pure silver.
11. **Discount Rate:** The "interest rate" the Fed charges banks to borrow money.
12. **Secondary Supply:** Metal that comes from melting old things (coins/jewelry) instead of from a mine.
13. **Intrinsic Value:** What something is worth "inside" (the metal itself), not just the number printed on it.
14. **Demonetization:** Deciding that a metal (like silver) is no longer "official money."
15. **Amalgamation:** Mixing silver ore with mercury to "catch" the silver.
16. **Token Currency:** Coins that are worth more because the government says so, not because of the metal inside.
17. **Gold Exchange Standard:** A system where paper money is backed by gold "somewhere else."
18. **Brazing:** Using melted silver to "glue" two pieces of metal together.
19. **Galena:** A heavy blue-grey rock that contains both lead and silver.
20. **Fiat Currency:** Money that is only valuable because the government "decrees" it so.
