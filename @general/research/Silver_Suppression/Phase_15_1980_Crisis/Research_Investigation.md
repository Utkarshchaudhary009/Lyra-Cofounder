## 4. Complex Words -> Simplification

1.  **Cornering the Market** -> Buying enough of something that you control the price because no one else has any to sell.
2.  **Margin Call** -> The bank calling you to say "Put more cash in your account immediately or we sell your house."
3.  **Liquidation Only** -> A rule where you are only allowed to exit the casino; you cannot sit down at a table to play.
4.  **Futures Contract** -> A paper promise to deliver silver later. Not the real metal.
5.  **Long Position** -> Betting the price will go up (buying).
6.  **Short Position** -> Betting the price will go down (selling).
7.  **Backwardation** -> When the price of a tomato today is more expensive than a tomato promised next month (means there is a shortage of tomatoes now).
8.  **Spot Price** -> The price to buy silver *right now* with cash.
9.  **Leverage** -> Using borrowed money to buy more stock/silver than you actually have cash for.
10. **Moral Suasion** -> When the Fed Chairman "strongly suggests" banks do something, and they do it out of fear, not law.
11. **Consortium** -> A group of banks teaming up to save their own skins.
12. **Exchange** -> The marketplace (like COMEX) where prices are set.
13. **Speculator** -> A gambler in the market (what they call you when you are winning).
14. **Hedger** -> Someone protecting themselves from price changes (what they call the banks).
15. **Volatility** -> How wildly the price swings up and down.
16. **Inflation** -> Money losing its value (making silver prices look higher).
17. **Fiat Currency** -> Paper money backed by nothing but faith in the government.
18. **Bullion** -> Physical bars or coins of precious metal.
19. **Brokerage** -> The middleman company that places your bets (e.g., Bache).
20. **Force Majeure** -> A clause that lets contract writers break their promises due to "unforeseeable events" (like losing too much money).
