### Clue 5: Mining Industry Recovery

**The Simple Truth:**  
You would think that if the price of silver dropped by 90%, miners would stop digging. They didn't. They dug *more*. Why? Because silver is often a "by-product." Miners digging for copper or lead find silver by accident. They sell it no matter what the price is to pay their bills. This "inelastic" supply keeps prices low because the supply doesn't shrink when prices fall.

**The Hard Evidence:**  
*   **Production 1980:** 341.4 million ounces.
*   **Production 1984:** **415.2 million ounces**.
*   **Trend:** Production *increased* by nearly **21%** while prices *crashed* by 80%.
*   **Mechanism:** Base metal mining (Copper/Zinc/Lead) ramped up, flooding the market with by-product silver.

**The Suppression Seed:**  
**By-product Inelasticity** is a cornerstone of suppression. It guarantees a constant flow of new physical silver into the market regardless of how low the price goes, preventing a natural supply crunch that would typically force prices back up.

### Clue 8: Silver Leasing Begins (The Birth of the Shadow Supply)

**The Simple Truth:**

*   **Double Counting:** The lessor (bank) books the silver as an asset; the lessee (industry) consumes it or sells it. The market "feels" like there is more silver than there actually is.

**The Suppression Seed:**  
**The Leasing Market** is the ultimate suppression tool. It creates artificial supply ("ghost silver") that satisfies demand without upward price pressure. It essentially executes a "naked short" selling strategy disguised as a banking product.

**The Hard Evidence:**
