### 7. Roll Market Manipulation: The Perpetual Kick
**The Simple Truth:** Short sellers never intend to deliver the silver. When their contract is about to expire, they "roll" it to the next month. They sell the expiring contract and buy a new one. This constant rolling keeps the short position alive forever.
**The Hard Evidence:**
*   **The Roll Yield:** Banks managed the spread between months to minimize the cost of rolling massive short positions.
*   **Open Interest:** Instead of declining at expiry (delivery), Open Interest often shifted en masse to the next month, "kicking the can" perpetually.
**The Suppression Seed:** Prevents true price discovery by avoiding the settlement of debts in physical metal.

### 8. Backwardation/Contango: The Broken Signal
**The Simple Truth:** Usually, future silver is cheaper than current silver (Contango). When future silver is *cheaper* (Backwardation), it means there is a desperate shortage RIGHT NOW. In silver, banks used paper selling to crush this signal.
**The Hard Evidence:**
*   **The Anomaly:** In **March 2020**, silver entered severe backwardation, yet the price fell to $12. A physical shortage signal was met with a paper sell-off—a mathematical impossibility in a free market.
**The Suppression Seed:** Destroying the market's "Check Engine Light" allows suppression to continue until the engine fails (force majeure).

### 9. Basis Trading: The Arbitrage Trap

**The Suppression Seed:** By controlling the arbitrage channel, banks isolated the physical price from the paper price (Bifurcation).
