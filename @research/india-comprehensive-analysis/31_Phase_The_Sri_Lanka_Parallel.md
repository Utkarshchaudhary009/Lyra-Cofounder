## 1. The Crash (2022): A Warning Bell
*   **The Event:** Sri Lanka ran out of dollars, defaulted on debt, and people stormed the President's palace.
*   **The Cause:** Decades of borrowing money to build useless projects + massive tax cuts + organic farming ban.
*   **The Parallel:** India's states also borrow heavily and promise freebies (Phase 1).

## 2. Debt Trap: Living on Credit
*   **Sri Lanka:** Debt-to-GDP reached 119%.
*   **India:** General Govt Debt is ~80-84%.
*   **The Lesson:** When you borrow to pay interest (not to build factories), you are on the road to Colombo.

## 3. Freebie Culture: Populism kills
*   **The Promise:** Politicians promised cheap rice and tax cuts to win votes.
*   **The Result:** The treasury went empty.
*   **India:** "Revdi Culture" (Free electricity, cash for votes) mimics this exact pattern.

## 4. The Organic Disaster: Policy Shock
*   **The Decision:** Overnight ban on fertilizers.
*   **The Result:** Crop yields crashed. Food prices soared.
*   **India:** Sudden policy shocks (Demonetization, Export bans) show similar "knee-jerk" governance risks.

## 5. The China Card: Hambantota
*   **The Deal:** Sri Lanka borrowed from China to build a port. Couldn't pay. China took the port.
*   **India:** We don't have Chinese debt, but our neighbors do, which compromises our security (Phase 21).

## 6. Family Politics: The Rajapaksas
*   **The Power:** One family controlled almost the entire government.
*   **The Corruption:** No checks and balances.
*   **India:** Dynastic politics and centralization of power (Theme 10) are similar risks.

## 7. Tourism Dependence: One-Legged Economy
*   **The Vulnerability:** Sri Lanka relied too much on Tourism. When COVID hit, income vanished.
*   **India:** We are more diversified, but our "Job Engine" is sputtering (Phase 6).

## 8. The Recovery (2025): A Painful Path
*   **The Status:** Sri Lanka is growing again (4% in 2025), but poverty is high.
*   **The Cost:** They had to raise taxes and cut spending (Austerity) to please the IMF.
*   **The Lesson:** The "Cure" for populism is extremely painful for the common man.

## 9. Brain Drain: The Exit
*   **The Exodus:** Professionals fled Sri Lanka during the crisis.
*   **India:** Our professionals are leaving *before* the crisis (Phase 15).

## 10. Synthesis: The Neighbor's House is Burning
1.  **Don't Borrow for Consumption:** Debt must build assets.
2.  **Don't Print Money:** It kills the currency.
3.  **Don't Elect Populists:** Freebies aren't free.
4.  **The Conclusion:** Sri Lanka is a "Trailer." If India's states don't stop their fiscal indiscipline, the movie plays here too.
