# Phase 41: Scenario A - The Middle Income Trap
*Theme: Scenario Planning (Future Paths)*

This document projects the "Middle Income Trap" scenario based on the real data gathered in Phases 35-50.

## 1. The Trap Defined
*   **Status:** India is a Lower Middle Income country (~$2,500 capita).
*   **The Risk:** Getting stuck here because we lose the "Low Cost" advantage (to Bangladesh/Vietnam - Phase 48) before gaining the "High Tech" advantage (Phase 2/37).

## 2. Evidence of the Trap (2024-25)
*   **Stagnant Exports:** Merchandise exports to GDP ratio is falling (Phase 48).
*   **Low Innovation:** R&D spend is flat at 0.64% (Phase 35).
*   **Jobless Growth:** High GDP (7%) but high Youth Unemployment (44%) (Phase 6).

## 3. The "Brazil" Path
*   **Trajectory:** Like Brazil, India risks becoming a high-cost, low-efficiency economy with massive inequality (Phase 17).
*   **Conclusion:** This is the most *likely* scenario given the current data. We will grow, but not fast enough to become "Rich."
