# Phase 46: The Power Sector (Darkness Ahead?)
*Theme: Specific Sectoral Rot*

This document uses 2024 debt and demand data to expose the fragility of India's power sector.

## 1. The Debt Mountain (₹7.5 Lakh Crore)
*   **Real Data (2024):** Total borrowings of DISCOMs rose to **₹7,526 Billion (₹7.52 Lakh Crore)** as of March 2024 (PFC Report).
*   **The Trend:** Despite multiple bailout packages (UDAY), debt continues to rise from ₹6.8 Lakh Crore in 2023.
*   **The Cause:** Selling power below cost to farmers/households and state governments failing to pay the subsidy bill.

## 2. Demand vs. Supply: The Peak
*   **The Record:** Peak power demand hit an all-time high of **250 GW** on May 30, 2024.
*   **The Success:** The shortage was only **0.1%** (a massive improvement from the past).
*   **The Risk:** This demand was met by flogging coal plants. The "Evening Peak" (when solar goes off) remains a critical vulnerability. Projections warn of a **15-20 GW evening shortfall** by 2027 if battery storage isn't built fast.

## 3. The Renewable Reality
*   **Capacity:** India ranks 3rd globally in renewable generation.
*   **The Gap:** While we add Solar/Wind, the Grid (transmission) is struggling to handle the fluctuation.
*   **Coal Dependence:** Coal generation grew by **2.8%** in 2024 to support the base load. We are not "phasing out" coal; we are dependent on it to keep the lights on.

## 4. Synthesis: A Financial House of Cards
1.  **Bankrupt Distributors:** DISCOMs cannot afford to buy 24/7 power or upgrade transformers.
2.  **Peak Stress:** The grid is running at maximum capacity. One major failure could cascade.
3.  **The Verdict:** The physics of the grid (250 GW met) is working for now, but the economics (₹7.5 Lakh Crore debt) is broken.
