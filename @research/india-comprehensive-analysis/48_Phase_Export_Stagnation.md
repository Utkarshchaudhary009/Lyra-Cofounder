# Phase 48: The Export Stagnation
*Theme: Specific Sectoral Rot*

This document contrasts India's export performance with Vietnam and Bangladesh using 2024 data.

## 1. The Textile Defeat
*   **Vietnam (2024):** Textile/Apparel exports reached **$44 Billion** (11% growth).
*   **Bangladesh (2024):** Apparel exports stood at **$46.9 Billion**.
*   **India (2024):** Apparel exports were just **$13.04 Billion**.
*   **The Comparison:** Vietnam and Bangladesh export **3.5x more clothing** than India. India has lost the labor-intensive manufacturing war.

## 2. Overall Export Stagnation
*   **Merchandise Exports:** Hovering around $430-450 Billion.
*   **Export/GDP Ratio:** While total trade (including imports) is ~29%, merchandise exports as a % of GDP have stagnated or fallen from the 2014 peak (14.7%).
*   **US Imports (Jan-July 2025):** US imports from Vietnam ($10.4B) and Bangladesh ($5.1B) grew, while India ($6.2B) trails Vietnam significantly.

## 3. The "Service" Crutch
*   **The Offset:** India relies entirely on Services Exports (IT/GCC) to reduce the trade deficit.
*   **The Risk:** Merchandise (Goods) creates jobs for the poor (Phases 2/6). Services create jobs for the English-speaking elite. Stagnation in goods exports means stagnation in mass employment.

## 4. Synthesis: Missing the Global Bus
1.  **Competitiveness:** We are more expensive than Vietnam (Logistics/Power).
2.  **Scale:** We lack the massive factories of Bangladesh.
3.  **The Verdict:** "Make in India" has worked for phones (assembly) but failed for traditional labor-intensive sectors like textiles, costing millions of potential jobs.
