# Phase 49: The Environmental Time Bomb
*Theme: Specific Sectoral Rot*

This document quantifies the economic loss from environmental degradation using 2024/2025 reports.

## 1. The GDP Cost of Pollution (9.5%)
*   **Real Data (Lancet 2025):** Pollution costs India **$339.4 Billion** annually.
*   **Percentage:** This equates to **9.5% of GDP**.
*   **Comparison:** World Bank estimated 1.36% loss in 2019, but newer models show the damage is far worse.
*   **Health Toll:** 1.7 Million deaths annually are attributed to air pollution (18% of all deaths).

## 2. Desertification (30% of Land)
*   **The Statistic:** ISRO's Space Applications Centre (2021/2024 data) reports **29.7%** of India's land is undergoing degradation/desertification.
*   **Recent Estimate:** Up to **120 million hectares** (33%) are affected.
*   **Impact:** This threatens food security for a population of 1.4 billion.

## 3. Heatwaves & Productivity
*   **The Risk:** India loses **5-10% of working hours** to heat stress.
*   **Sector Hit:** Agriculture and Construction (where most poor people work) are hit hardest. You cannot work outside in 48°C heat.

## 4. Synthesis: Burning the Future
1.  **Economic Drag:** Losing 9.5% of GDP to pollution wipes out our 7% growth. We are effectively running in place.
2.  **Health Crisis:** Pollution is a "Mass Killer."
3.  **The Verdict:** Environmental destruction is not an "externality"; it is the single biggest tax on the Indian economy.
