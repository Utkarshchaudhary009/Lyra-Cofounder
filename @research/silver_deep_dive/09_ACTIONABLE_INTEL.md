# Phase 9: "What To Do" - Actionable Intelligence
*The Playbook for Ownership, Storage, and Exit*

## Introduction
Knowing the history and the physics of silver is intellectual entertainment. Owning it is financial survival. This phase moves from theory to practice. It answers the critical questions: What should I buy? Where should I put it? How do I sell it without getting wrecked by taxes or premiums?

## 1. What to Buy: The Hierarchy of Metal
Not all silver is created equal.
*   **For Barter & Survival (SHTF):** Buy **"Junk Silver"** (Pre-1965 US Dimes, Quarters, Half-Dollars).
    *   *Why?* Everyone recognizes them. They are 90% silver. They are fractional (small change). You can buy a loaf of bread with a mercury dime; you can't buy it with a 100oz bar.
*   **For Wealth Preservation:** Buy **Sovereign Coins** (American Eagles, Canadian Maples).
    *   *Why?* They are the most liquid. In a crisis, a dealer will buy a Maple Leaf instantly. A generic "round" might need to be tested or assayed first.
*   **For Pure Weight:** Buy **10oz or 100oz Bars**.
    *   *Why?* Lowest premiums. You get the most silver for your dollar. Avoid 1,000oz bars unless you are an institution; they are hard to sell quickly.

## 2. Avoiding Fakes: The "Alibaba" Threat
Counterfeit silver is flooding the market.
*   **The Magnet Test:** Silver is diamagnetic. If a magnet sticks to it, it's fake. If a magnet slides down it *too fast*, it's fake (it should slide slowly due to eddy currents).
*   **The Ice Test:** Silver melts ice instantly (highest thermal conductivity).
*   **The Ping Test:** Real silver rings like a bell (high-pitched, long sustain). Fake silver goes "thud."
*   **Rule #1:** Never buy from Chinese marketplaces (Alibaba/Temu) or unverified eBay sellers. Stick to major, reputable dealers.

## 3. Storage: The "Midnight Gardening" Problem
*   **Home Storage:** Great for access, bad for security.
    *   *The Trap:* Standard homeowner's insurance typically only covers ~$200 of bullion. You need a specific "rider" or separate insurance policy.
*   **Offshore Storage:** For large amounts, get it out of the banking system.
    *   *Top Jurisdictions:* **Singapore** and **Switzerland**. Look for "Non-Bank" vaults.
    *   *Allocated vs. Unallocated:* **ALWAYS** choose "Allocated" and "Segregated." If it is unallocated, you are an unsecured creditor of the vault.

## 4. Paper Substitutes: Proceed with Caution
*   **PSLV vs. SLV:**
    *   **SLV:** Designed for traders. Hard to redeem for physical metal. Authorized Participants (banks) run the show.
    *   **PSLV:** Closed-end fund. Holds fully allocated bars. Retail investors *can* redeem for physical (minimum amounts apply). Generally considered safer.
*   **Miners vs. Streamers:**
    *   *Miners:* High risk (fuel costs, strikes, nationalization).
    *   *Streamers (WPM, FNV):* They give cash to miners upfront for the right to buy silver cheap later. Lower risk, high margins.

## 5. The Exit: Taxes & Laws
*   **Capital Gains:** In the US, silver is taxed as a **"Collectible."** The long-term rate is **28%**, not the usual 15-20% for stocks.
*   **Reporting (1099-B):** Dealers are required by law to report your sale to the IRS if you sell:
    *   1,000 oz of silver bars or more.
    *   $1,000 face value of Junk Silver.
    *   *Loophole:* Currently, sales of American Silver Eagles are often exempt from this *automatic* dealer reporting (though you are still legally required to self-report taxes).
