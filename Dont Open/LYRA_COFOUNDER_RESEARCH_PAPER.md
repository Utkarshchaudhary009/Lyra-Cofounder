Relationships**- Prior connection and trust foundation- Complementary working styles- Shared values and vision alignment- Communication compatibility- Conflict resolution capability**2. Roles**- Clear division of responsibilities- Complementary skill sets (not redundant)- Defined decision-making authority- Adaptive role evolution capacity- Non-overlapping expertise zones**3. Rewards**- Fair equity distribution- Dynamic adjustment mechanisms- Contribution-based compensation- Long-term value alignment- Exit strategy clarity> *"Nothing can bedevil a high-potential startup more than its 'people problems.' These problems typically result from choices that founders make as they add team members."* — Noam Wasserman, Harvard Business School### 1.2 The Founder's Dilemma: Rich vs. KingWasserman's research identifies a critical trade-off every founder faces:| Dimension | "Rich" Path | "King" Path ||-----------|-------------|-------------|| **Focus** | Financial wealth maximization | Control & decision-making power || **Team Approach** | Brings in best talent regardless of control loss | Maintains tight control, limits external talent || **Equity Strategy** | Shares equity to attract resources | Retains maximum equity || **Outcome Pattern** | Higher company value, lower personal ownership | Lower company value, higher personal control |**Key Finding:** Founders who prioritize attracting the best resources (including cofounders) over maintaining control create more valuable companies, even if they own smaller stakes.### 1.3 Cofounder Selection Criteria (Stanford GSB
