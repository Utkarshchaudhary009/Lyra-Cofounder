## 📗 Set A: Board Blitz — Solutions (30 Questions)

### A1: Mass Percentage of NaOH Solution
**Given:** 10 g NaOH dissolved in 90 g water

**Solution:**
Total mass of solution = mass of solute + mass of solvent = 10 g + 90 g = 100 g

Mass percentage = (mass of solute / total mass of solution) × 100
$$\\text{Mass %} = \\frac{10}{100} \\times 100 = 10\\%$$

**Answer:** 10% (or 10% w/w)

---

### A2: Molarity of H₂SO₄ Solution
**Given:** 4.9 g H₂SO₄ in 250 mL solution, M = 98 g/mol

**Solution:**
Moles of H₂SO₄ = 4.9 g ÷ 98 g/mol = 0.05 mol
Volume in liters = 250 mL = 0.250 L

Molarity = moles ÷ volume (L)
$$M = \\frac{0.05}{0.250} = 0.2 \\text{ M}$$

**Answer:** 0.2 M

---

### A3: Molality of Urea Solution
**Given:** 6 g urea (M = 60 g/mol) in 500 g water

**Solution:**
Moles of urea = 6 g ÷ 60 g/mol = 0.1 mol
Mass of solvent in kg = 500 g = 0.5 kg

Molality = moles of solute ÷ mass of solvent (kg)
$$m = \\frac{0.1}{0.5} = 0.2 \\text{ m}$$

**Answer:** 0.2 m

---

### A4: Mole Fraction of Water
**Given:** χ_ethanol = 0.05 in ethanol-water solution

**Solution:**
In a binary solution: χ_solute + χ_solvent = 1
Therefore: χ_water = 1 - χ_ethanol = 1 - 0.05 = 0.95

**Answer:** 0.95

### A5: Salt Content in Seawater

**Solution:**

**Answer:** 70 g

### A6: Molality from Mass Percentage
**Given:** 5% glucose (M = 180 g/mol) by mass

**Solution:**

- Mass of glucose = 5 g
- Mass of water = 95 g = 0.095 kg

Moles of glucose = 5 g ÷ 180 g/mol = 0.0278 mol

Molality = 0.0278 mol ÷ 0.095 kg = 0.292 m

**Answer:** 0.292 m (or 0.29 m to 2 sig figs)

### A7: Dilution of HCl
**Given:** 100 mL of 1 M HCl diluted to 500 mL

**Solution:**
Using dilution formula: M₁V₁ = M₂V₂
$$1 \\times 100 = M_2 \\times 500$$
$$M_2 = \\frac{100}{500} = 0.2 \\text{ M}$$

**Answer:** 0.2 M

### A8: ppm to Mass Percent
**Given:** 2 ppm fluoride

**Solution:**
pm means parts per million by mass.
1 ppm = 1 mg solute / 1 kg solution = 10⁻⁶ (mass ratio)

2 ppm = 2 × 10⁻⁶ = 2 × 10⁻⁴ %
$$2 \\text{ ppm} = \\frac{2}{10^6} \\times 100\\% = 0.0002\\%$$

**Answer:** 0.0002% or 2 × 10⁻⁴ %

### A9: Normality of H₂SO₄

**Solution:**
Normality = n × Molarity
$$N = 2 \\times 0.5 = 1 \\text{ N}$$

**Answer:** 1 N

### A11: Mole Fraction of NaCl
**Given:** 11.7 g NaCl (M = 58.5 g/mol) in 250 g water

**Solution:**
Moles of NaCl = 11.7 ÷ 58.5 = 0.2 mol
Moles of H₂O = 250 ÷ 18 = 13.889 mol
Total moles = 0.2 + 13.889 = 14.089 mol

$$\\chi_{NaCl} = \\frac{0.2}{14.089} = 0.0142$$

**Answer:** 0.0142 (or 0.014)

### A13: Neutralization of NaOH and HCl
**Given:** 50 mL of 2 M NaOH + 50 mL of 2 M HCl

**Solution:**
Moles of NaOH = 2 × 0.050 = 0.1 mol
Moles of HCl = 2 × 0.050 = 0.1 mol

Reaction: NaOH + HCl → NaCl + H₂O
1:1 stoichiometry, so complete neutralization.

Final solution contains:
- NaCl: 0.1 mol in 100 mL = 1 M
- Water (excess from solvent)
- No excess H⁺ or OH⁻

**Answer:** Resulting solution is 1 M NaCl with neutral pH (pH = 7)

### A18: Titration Volume
**Given:** 25 mL of 0.1 M NaOH neutralized by 0.1 M HCl

**Solution:**
Moles of NaOH = 0.1 × 0.025 = 0.0025 mol
For neutralization: moles HCl = moles NaOH
$$0.1 \\times V = 0.0025$$
$$V = 0.025 \\text{ L} = 25 \\text{ mL}$$

**Answer:** 25 mL

### A19: Ion Concentrations in Na₂SO₄
**Given:** 0.1 M Na₂SO₄

**Solution:**
Na₂SO₄ → 2Na⁺ + SO₄²⁻
$$[Na^+] = 2 \\times 0.1 = 0.2 \\text{ M}$$
$$[SO_4^{2-}] = 0.1 \\text{ M}$$

Charge balance verification:
Positive charge: 0.2 M × (+1) = +0.2
Negative charge: 0.1 M × (-2) = -0.2
Net charge = 0 ✓

**Answer:** [Na⁺] = 0.2 M, [SO₄²⁻] = 0.1 M, charge balanced

### A21: Molality from Mole Fraction
**Given:** χ_solute = 0.02, M_water = 18 g/mol

**Solution:**
If χ_solute = 0.02, then χ_water = 0.98

Mole ratio: n_solute/n_water = 0.02/0.98 = 0.0204

For 1 kg (1000 g) water: n_water = 1000/18 = 55.56 mol
n_solute = 0.0204 × 55.56 = 1.133 mol

Molality = 1.133 m

**Answer:** 1.13 m

$$M_{final} = \\frac{1.2}{0.5} = 2.4 \\text{ M}$$

**Answer:** 2.4 M

### A28: Molality from Mole Fraction
**Given:** χ_benzene = 0.2 in CCl₄, M_CCl₄ = 154 g/mol

**Solution:**
If χ_benzene = 0.2, then χ_CCl₄ = 0.8

Mole ratio: n_benzene/n_CCl₄ = 0.2/0.8 = 0.25

For 1 kg (1000 g) CCl₄: n_CCl₄ = 1000/154 = 6.494 mol
n_benzene = 0.25 × 6.494 = 1.623 mol

Molality = 1.62 m

**Answer:** 1.62 m

### A30: Dilution and Titration
**Given:** 25 mL of 12 M HCl diluted to 500 mL, then titrated with 10 mL of 1 M NaOH

**Solution:**
Part (a): M₁V₁ = M₂V₂
$$12 \\times 25 = M_2 \\times 500$$
$$M_2 = \\frac{300}{500} = 0.6 \\text{ M}$$

Part (b): Moles NaOH = 1 × 0.010 = 0.01 mol
For neutralization: moles HCl needed = 0.01 mol
$$0.6 \\times V = 0.01$$
$$V = 0.01667 \\text{ L} = 16.67 \\text{ mL}$$

**Answer:** (a) 0.6 M, (b) 16.67 mL
