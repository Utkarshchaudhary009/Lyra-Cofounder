<summary>💡 Detailed Solutions for Set B: JEE Foundation</summary>

**B1:**
*   **Calculation:** Assume $100\text{ g}$ solution. mass NaOH = $10\text{ g}$ ($0.25\text{ mol}$), mass water = $90\text{ g}$ ($0.090\text{ kg}$, $5.0\text{ mol}$). Vol = $100 / 1.11 = 90.09\text{ mL} = 0.09009\text{ L}$.
    (a) $M = 0.25 / 0.09009 = 2.775\text{ M}$. 
    (b) $m = 0.25 / 0.090 = 2.778\text{ m}$. 
    (c) $\chi = 0.25 / (0.25 + 5.0) = 0.0476$.
*   **Answer:** (a) $2.78\text{ M}$, (b) $2.78\text{ m}$, (c) $0.0476$

**B2:**
*   **Calculation:** Assume $1\text{ L}$ solution. Moles urea = $3\text{ mol}$ ($180\text{ g}$). Mass solution = $1000 \times 1.07 = 1070\text{ g}$. Mass water = $1070 - 180 = 890\text{ g} = 0.89\text{ kg}$.
    (a) $m = 3 / 0.89 = 3.37\text{ m}$. 
    (b) Mass \% = $(180 / 1070) \times 100 = 16.82\%$.
*   **Answer:** (a) $3.37\text{ m}$, (b) $16.82\%$

**B3:**
*   **Calculation:** $\chi_{\text{EtOH}} = 0.15 \Rightarrow$ ratio is $15\text{ mol EtOH} : 85\text{ mol H}_2\text{O}$ or $3:17$.
    Let $n_{\text{H}_2\text{O}} = 17\text{ mol}$ ($306\text{ g}$) and $n_{\text{EtOH}} = 3\text{ mol}$ ($138\text{ g}$). Total mass = $444\text{ g}$.
    (a) $m = \frac{n_{\text{solute}}}{\text{kg solvent}} = 3 / 0.306 = 9.80\text{ m}$.
    (b) Vol = $444 / 0.97 = 457.7\text{ mL} = 0.4577\text{ L}$. $M = 3 / 0.4577 = 6.55\text{ M}$.
*   **Answer:** (a) $9.80\text{ m}$, (b) $6.55\text{ M}$

**B4:**
*   **Calculation:** $\text{H}_2\text{SO}_4$ moles = $0.2 \times 0.5 = 0.10\text{ mol}$. NaOH moles = $0.3 \times 0.4 = 0.12\text{ mol}$.
    $\text{H}_2\text{SO}_4 + 2\text{NaOH} \rightarrow \text{Na}_2\text{SO}_4 + 2\text{H}_2\text{O}$.
    Need $2\text{ mol NaOH}$ per $1\text{ mol H}_2\text{SO}_4$. So $0.12\text{ mol NaOH}$ reacts with exactly $0.06\text{ mol H}_2\text{SO}_4$.
    Excess $\text{H}_2\text{SO}_4$ = $0.10 - 0.06 = 0.04\text{ mol}$. Final Vol = $500\text{ mL} = 0.5\text{ L}$.
    $M(\text{excess}) = 0.04 / 0.5 = 0.08\text{ M}$.
*   **Answer:** $\text{H}_2\text{SO}_4$ is in excess, concentration is $0.08\text{ M}$

**D1:**
*   **Calculation:** Let solution mass be $W_{sol}$. $V_{sol} = W_{sol}/d$. Let moles of solute be $n$. $M = n/V_{sol} = (n \cdot d)/W_{sol} \times 1000$. Molality $m = (n/W_{solvent}) \times 1000$. We know $W_{solvent} = W_{sol} - n \cdot M_{solute}$. So $W_{sol} = W_{solvent} + n \cdot M_{solute}$.
    Substitute $W_{solvent} = 1000n/m$ to get $W_{sol} = 1000n/m + n \cdot M_{solute} = n(1000/m + M_{solute})$.
    Thus, $V_{sol} = n(1000/m + M_{solute})/d$. By definition, $M = n \cdot 1000 / V_{sol}$. Substitute $V_{sol}$: $M = \frac{n \cdot 1000}{n(1000/m + M_{solute})/d} = \frac{1000d}{1000/m + M_{solute}}$.
    Rearrange for $d$: $d = M \left( \frac{1000/m + M_{solute}}{1000} \right) = M \left( \frac{1}{m} + \frac{M_{solute}}{1000} \right) = \frac{M(1000 + m \cdot M_{solute})}{1000m}$.
    If $M = m$, $d = \frac{m(1000 + m \cdot M_{solute})}{1000m} = 1 + \frac{m \cdot M_{solute}}{1000} = 1 + \frac{M \cdot M_{solute}}{1000}$.
    **Physical Meaning:** When $M = m$, the volume of the solution in liters is exactly equal to the mass of the solvent in kg. This implies the mass of the solute does not subtract from the solvent volume capacity. Density is simply $1$ (for the solvent assuming water) plus the additional mass of the solute normalized per liter.
*   **Answer:** Derivation yields $d = 1 + \frac{M \cdot M_{solute}}{1000}$ when $M=m$. Signifies solvent volume approximates solution volume.

    (2) $w/w\% \rightarrow m$: Moles $= w/M_{solute}$. Mass solvent $= (100-w)/1000\text{ kg}$. $m = (w/M_{solute}) \times 1000 / (100-w)$.
    (3) $w/w\% \rightarrow \chi$: $\chi = (w/M_{solute}) / [ (w/M_{solute}) + ((100-w)/M_{solvent}) ]$.
    (4) $m \rightarrow M$: Start with 1 kg solvent. $m$ moles solute. Mass solution $= 1000 + m \cdot M_{solute}$. Vol $= (1000 + m \cdot M_{solute})/d$. $M = 1000 \cdot m \cdot d / (1000 + m \cdot M_{solute})$.
    (5) $\chi \rightarrow m$: Ratio definition. $m = (\chi / (1-\chi)) \times 1000 / M_{solvent}$.
    (6) $\chi \rightarrow M$: Combine (5) and (4).
*   **Answer:** Derived all six key relationship formulas algebraically.

**D10:**
*   **Calculation:** 
    (a) $0.9\% \text{ w/v}$ means $0.9\text{ g}$ NaCl per $100\text{ mL}$ solution. In $10\text{ L} (10000\text{ mL})$, mass $= 0.9 \times 100 = 90\text{ g}$ NaCl.
    (b) Moles NaCl $= 90\text{ g} / 58.5\text{ g/mol} = 1.538$ mol. Molarity $= 1.538\text{ mol} / 10\text{ L} = 0.1538\text{ M}$.
    (c) Osmolarity $= M \times i = 0.1538 \times 2 = 0.307\text{ Osm/L} = 307\text{ mOsm/L}$.
    (d) $307\text{ mOsm/L}$ closely matches the natural osmolarity of human blood plasma (approx. $290-310\text{ mOsm/L}$). Being isotonic means it exerts the same osmotic pressure as intracellular fluids, preventing cells from bursting or shrinking upon administration.
*   **Answer:** (a) $90\text{ g}$, (b) $0.1538\text{ M}$, (c) $307\text{ mOsm/L}$, (d) prevents osmotic lysis/crenation of blood cells.
