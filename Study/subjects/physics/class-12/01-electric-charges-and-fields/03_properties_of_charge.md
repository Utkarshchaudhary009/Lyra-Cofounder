# Chapter 3: Properties of Electric Charge

> *NCERT Section 1.5*

## рҹҺҜ Stage 1: The Core Idea

Electric charge has three fundamental properties. Think of them as the *rules of the game* вҖ” every problem in electrostatics obeys these, no exceptions.

### Property 1: Additivity of Charges

Charges add up **algebraically** (not like scalars that are always positive).

If a system has charges qвӮҒ, qвӮӮ, qвӮғ, ..., qвӮҷ, then:

$$Q_{total} = q_1 + q_2 + q_3 + \cdots + q_n$$

The signs matter. +5 ОјC and вҲ’3 ОјC together give +2 ОјC, not 8 ОјC.

> **Analogy:** Think of charge like money in a bank account. Deposits are positive, withdrawals are negative. Your balance is the algebraic sum.

### Property 2: Conservation of Charge

The total charge of an **isolated system** never changes. Charge cannot be created or destroyed вҖ” only transferred.

This is one of the most fundamental conservation laws in physics, right alongside conservation of energy and momentum.

**Examples in nature:**
- Rubbing: Glass (+Q) + Silk (вҲ’Q) = 0 вң“
- Pair production: Оі вҶ’ eвҒ» + eвҒә (0 вҶ’ вҲ’e + e = 0) вң“
- ОІ-decay: n вҶ’ p + eвҒ» + ОҪМ„вӮ‘ (0 вҶ’ +1 вҲ’ 1 + 0 = 0) вң“

### Property 3: Quantization of Charge вӯҗ

Charge comes in discrete packets. The smallest unit is:

$$e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$$

Any charge Q must be:

$$Q = ne \quad \text{where } n = 0, \pm1, \pm2, \pm3, \ldots$$

> **Analogy:** You can buy 1 egg, 2 eggs, 10 eggs вҖ” but never 1.5 eggs. Similarly, charge is "quantized" into indivisible units of *e*.

**Why don't we notice quantization in daily life?**

Because *e* is incredibly small. A charge of 1 ОјC = 10вҒ»вҒ¶ C contains about 6.25 Г— 10В№ВІ elementary charges. At this scale, the "graininess" is invisible вҖ” like how sand looks smooth from an airplane.

> **At the macroscopic level, charge appears continuous. At the microscopic level, it's discrete.** This is why we can use continuous charge distributions (Chapter 9) without guilt.

## рҹ”¬ Stage 2: The Formula Lab

### Formula 1: Additivity

$$Q_{total} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} q_i$$

- Charges are **algebraic** вҖ” include signs.
- Works for any system: point charges, distributed charges, etc.

### Formula 2: Conservation

$$Q_{before} = Q_{after} \quad \text{(for an isolated system)}$$

- "Isolated" means no charge enters or leaves the system boundary.
- This holds in ALL physical processes: chemical, nuclear, electromagnetic.

### Formula 3: Quantization

$$Q = ne$$

| Variable | Meaning | Unit |
|----------|---------|------|
| Q | Total charge | Coulomb (C) |
| n | Integer (positive, negative, or zero) | dimensionless |
| e | Elementary charge = 1.6 Г— 10вҒ»В№вҒ№ C | Coulomb (C) |

**Note on quarks:** Quarks carry charges of Вұe/3 or Вұ2e/3, but they are **never observed in isolation** (confinement). So for all practical and exam purposes, e is the minimum free charge.

### Type 2: Conservation in contact problems вӯҗ

**Pattern:** "Two charged bodies touch and separate."

**Solved Example** рҹҹЎ

> Sphere A (+8 ОјC) and sphere B (вҲ’2 ОјC) are identical conducting spheres. They touch and separate. Find the charge on each.

**Solution:**
- Total charge = +8 + (вҲ’2) = +6 ОјC (conserved)
- Identical spheres share equally: Each = +6/2 = **+3 ОјC**

**Practice:**

1. рҹҹў Spheres with +10 ОјC and вҲ’4 ОјC (identical) touch and separate. *(Ans: +3 ОјC each)*
2. рҹҹЎ Sphere A (+6 ОјC, radius R) and Sphere B (вҲ’2 ОјC, radius 2R) touch and separate. Find charges. *(Hint: Charge distributes in ratio of radii for unequal spheres: q_A/q_B = R_A/R_B)*

<details>
<summary><b>Answer to Q2</b></summary>

Total = +4 ОјC. Ratio = R : 2R = 1 : 2.
q_A = 4 Г— (1/3) = **4/3 ОјC**
q_B = 4 Г— (2/3) = **8/3 ОјC**
</details>

3. рҹ”ҙ вӯҗ Sphere A (+Q) touches B (neutral, identical). B then touches C (neutral, identical). Then C touches A. Find final charge on each.

<details>
<summary><b>Answer</b></summary>

Step 1: A touches B вҶ’ each gets Q/2. A = Q/2, B = Q/2.
Step 2: B touches C вҶ’ each gets Q/4. B = Q/4, C = Q/4.
Step 3: C touches A вҶ’ Total = Q/2 + Q/4 = 3Q/4. Each gets 3Q/8.
A = 3Q/8, C = 3Q/8.

**Final: A = 3Q/8, B = Q/4, C = 3Q/8**
Total = 3Q/8 + Q/4 + 3Q/8 = 3Q/8 + 2Q/8 + 3Q/8 = 8Q/8 = Q вң“
</details>

### Type 3: Conservation in nuclear/particle reactions

**Solved Example** рҹҹЎ

> In ОІвҒ» decay, a neutron transforms as: n вҶ’ p + eвҒ» + ОҪМ„вӮ‘. Verify conservation of charge.

**Solution:**
- Before: charge of neutron = 0
- After: charge of proton (+e) + electron (вҲ’e) + antineutrino (0) = 0
- 0 = 0 вң“ **Charge is conserved.**

**Practice:**

1. рҹҹў Verify charge conservation in pair production: Оі вҶ’ eвҒ» + eвҒә *(Ans: 0 = вҲ’e + e = 0 вң“)*
2. рҹҹЎ In pair annihilation: eвҒ» + eвҒә вҶ’ 2Оі. Is charge conserved? *(Ans: (вҲ’e + e) = 0, and 2Оі = 0. Yes вң“)*
3. рҹҹЎ In nuclear fission: ВІВівҒөU + n вҶ’ В№вҒҙвҒҙBa + вҒёвҒ№Kr + 3n. Verify charge conservation. *(Ans: 92 + 0 = 56 + 36 + 0 = 92 вң“)*

### Type 6: Additivity with vector-like sign considerations

**Solved Example** рҹҹЎ

> A hydrogen atom has 1 proton and 1 electron. What is the total charge? A helium atom has 2 protons, 2 neutrons, and 2 electrons. Total charge?

**Solution:**
- H: +e + (вҲ’e) = **0**
- He: 2(+e) + 2(0) + 2(вҲ’e) = **0**

All neutral atoms have zero net charge.

**Practice:**

2. рҹҹў An OВІвҒ» ion has 8 protons and 10 electrons. Charge? *(Ans: вҲ’2e = вҲ’3.2 Г— 10вҒ»В№вҒ№ C)*
3. рҹҹЎ In 1 mole of NaCl, what is the total charge? *(Ans: 0 вҖ” equal NaвҒә and ClвҒ» ions)*

### Type 7: Charge conservation in chemical reactions

**Solved Example** рҹҹЎ

### Type 8: Conceptual questions on properties

**Solved Example** рҹҹЎ

> "Charge is conserved" and "charge is quantized" вҖ” are these independent properties? Can a system violate one while obeying the other?

**Solution:**

These are **independent** properties:
- **Conservation** is about the total quantity remaining constant.
- **Quantization** is about the charge coming in discrete units.

In principle, you could have a universe where charge is conserved but not quantized (continuous charge, always conserved). Or quantized but not conserved (discrete chunks that appear/disappear). In *our* universe, both hold simultaneously.

**Practice:**

1. рҹҹЎ "At the macroscopic level, quantization of charge can be ignored." Justify. *(Ans: Because e is so small, macroscopic charges contain ~10В№ВІвҒә quanta, making charge appear continuous)*
2. рҹҹЎ Does conservation of charge hold for non-isolated systems? *(Ans: No вҖ” charge can flow in/out. It holds only for the total isolated system.)*
3. рҹҹЎ A proton and an antiproton annihilate to produce two gamma ray photons. Is charge conserved? *(Ans: Yes. +e + (вҲ’e) = 0, and Оі photons have zero charge.)*

## рҹ”Җ Stage 4: Type Mixer

**Q1.** рҹҹЎ вӯҗ Three identical spheres A, B, C have charges +6 ОјC, вҲ’2 ОјC, and +4 ОјC. A and B touch first and separate. Then B and C touch and separate. Find final charges on A, B, C. Verify conservation.

<details>
<summary><b>Solution</b></summary>

**Step 1:** A (+6) touches B (вҲ’2) вҶ’ Total = 4 ОјC вҶ’ Each gets +2 ОјC.
A = +2 ОјC, B = +2 ОјC, C = +4 ОјC.

**Step 2:** B (+2) touches C (+4) вҶ’ Total = 6 ОјC вҶ’ Each gets +3 ОјC.
A = +2 ОјC, B = +3 ОјC, C = +3 ОјC.

**Check:** 2 + 3 + 3 = 8 ОјC. Original: 6 + (вҲ’2) + 4 = 8 ОјC вң“ **Conserved.**
</details>

**Q2.** рҹ”ҙ A body has 10ВІвҒ° protons and 10ВІвҒ° + 3 Г— 10В№ВІ electrons. Find (a) the net charge on the body, (b) is this charge quantized? (c) what fraction of total electrons constitutes the excess?

<details>
<summary><b>Solution</b></summary>

(a) Excess electrons = 3 Г— 10В№ВІ
Q = вҲ’ne = вҲ’3 Г— 10В№ВІ Г— 1.6 Г— 10вҒ»В№вҒ№ = **вҲ’4.8 Г— 10вҒ»вҒ· C = вҲ’0.48 ОјC**

(b) n = 3 Г— 10В№ВІ, an integer вҶ’ **Yes, quantized** вң“

(c) Fraction = 3 Г— 10В№ВІ / 10ВІвҒ° = **3 Г— 10вҒ»вҒё = 0.000003%**

> This tiny imbalance produces a measurable charge вҖ” showing how sensitive electrostatics is.
</details>

**Q3.** рҹҹЎ When 10В№вҒҙ electrons are transferred from body A to body B:

(b) A loses 10В№вҒҙ electrons вҶ’ Q_A = **+16 ОјC**

## рҹ“Ӣ Stage 5: Board Arsenal

**Q1.** рҹҹў вӯҗ State the three basic properties of electric charge. *(3 marks)*

<details>
<summary><b>Model Answer</b></summary>

1. **Additivity:** The total charge of a system is the algebraic sum of all individual charges: Q = qвӮҒ + qвӮӮ + ... + qвӮҷ.

2. **Conservation:** The total charge of an isolated system remains constant. Charge is neither created nor destroyed, only transferred.

3. **Quantization:** Charge exists in discrete packets. The charge on any body is always an integral multiple of the elementary charge e = 1.6 Г— 10вҒ»В№вҒ№ C. That is, Q = ne, where n is an integer.
</details>

**Q2.** рҹҹЎ вӯҗ What is quantization of charge? Why is it not observed at the macroscopic level? *(3 marks)*

<details>
<summary><b>Model Answer</b></summary>

**Quantization of charge** means that the charge on any body can only take values that are integral multiples of the elementary charge *e* = 1.6 Г— 10вҒ»В№вҒ№ C. Mathematically, Q = ne, where n is an integer.

At the **macroscopic level**, the charges we deal with (microcoulombs, millicoulombs) involve an enormous number of elementary charges (~10В№ВІ or more). At this scale, the discrete nature of charge is imperceptible, and charge appears to be a continuous quantity вҖ” just as individual grains of sand are invisible when looking at a beach from a distance.
</details>

**Q3.** рҹҹЎ An ebonite rod is rubbed with fur. The rod acquires a charge of вҲ’4.8 Г— 10вҒ»вҒё C. (a) How many electrons were transferred and in which direction? (b) What charge does the fur acquire? *(3 marks)*

<details>
<summary><b>Model Answer</b></summary>

(a) n = Q/e = 4.8 Г— 10вҒ»вҒё / 1.6 Г— 10вҒ»В№вҒ№ = **3 Г— 10В№В№ electrons**
Direction: From fur to ebonite rod (rod gained electrons, so fur lost them).

(b) By conservation of charge: Charge on fur = **+4.8 Г— 10вҒ»вҒё C**
</details>

**Q4.** рҹҹў Is a charge of 2 Г— 10вҒ»В№вҒ№ C possible? Justify. *(1 mark)*

<details>
<summary><b>Model Answer</b></summary>

n = 2 Г— 10вҒ»В№вҒ№ / 1.6 Г— 10вҒ»В№вҒ№ = 1.25

Since n is not an integer, this charge is **not possible** according to the quantization of charge.
</details>

**Q3.** рҹ”ҙ вӯҗ Two identical conducting spheres with charges +6Q and вҲ’2Q are brought into contact and separated. The electrostatic force between them at distance *d* after separation, compared to the force before contact, changes by a factor of:

<details>
<summary><b>Answer</b></summary>

**Before contact:** FвӮҒ = k(6Q)(2Q)/dВІ = 12kQВІ/dВІ

**After contact:** Each gets (6Q вҲ’ 2Q)/2 = 2Q.
FвӮӮ = k(2Q)(2Q)/dВІ = 4kQВІ/dВІ

Ratio = FвӮӮ/FвӮҒ = 4/12 = **1/3**

**Answer: (b)**
</details>

**Q4.** рҹ”ҙ A certain charge Q is divided into two parts: q and (Q вҲ’ q). For the two parts to have maximum Coulomb repulsion at a fixed separation, q should be:

For maximum F: dF/dq = k(Q вҲ’ 2q)/rВІ = 0 вҶ’ **q = Q/2**

**Answer: (b)**
</details>
