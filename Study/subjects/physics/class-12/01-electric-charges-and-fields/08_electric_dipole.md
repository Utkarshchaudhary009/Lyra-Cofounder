## рҹҺҜ Stage 1: The Core Idea

### What Is an Electric Dipole?

An electric dipole is the **simplest arrangement of charges that has no net charge but still produces an electric field.** It consists of:

- Two equal and opposite charges: **+q** and **вҲ’q**
- Separated by a small distance **2a**

> **Analogy:** Think of a bar magnet вҖ” it has a North and South pole that are inseparable. Similarly, a dipole has a + and вҲ’ end. You can't have one without the other. Many molecules (like water, HCl) are natural dipoles.

### Dipole Moment вӯҗ

The **electric dipole moment** pвғ— is:

$$\vec{p} = q \times 2a \times \hat{n}$$

Where nМӮ is the unit vector from вҲ’q to +q.

**Direction:** Always from **negative to positive** charge.

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Magnitude | p = q Г— 2a |
| SI Unit | CВ·m (coulomb-metre) |
| Debye (non-SI) | 1 D = 3.33 Г— 10вҒ»ВівҒ° CВ·m |
| Direction | вҲ’q вҶ’ +q |

> Dipole moment is a **vector**. Its direction matters enormously.

### Why Dipoles Matter

1. **Water molecule** is a dipole (p = 6.17 Г— 10вҒ»ВівҒ° CВ·m) вҖ” this is why water dissolves salts.
2. **Antennas** are oscillating dipoles.
3. Dipole fields fall as **1/rВі** (faster than point charge fields at 1/rВІ) вҖ” they're short-range.
4. In board/JEE exams, dipole questions account for **~25% of all marks** from this chapter.

## рҹ”¬ Stage 2: The Formula Lab

### Formula 1: Electric Field on the Axial Line (End-on) вӯҗвӯҗ

Point P is at distance r from the center of the dipole, along the dipole axis.

$$E_{axial} = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \cdot \frac{2pr}{(r^2 - a^2)^2}$$

**For r >> a (short dipole / far-field):**

$$\boxed{E_{axial} = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \cdot \frac{2p}{r^3}}$$

**Direction:** Along the dipole moment (from вҲ’q to +q) вҶ’ **same direction as pвғ—**

### Formula 2: Electric Field on the Equatorial Line (Broadside-on) вӯҗвӯҗ

Point P is at distance r from the center, perpendicular to the dipole axis.

$$E_{eq} = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \cdot \frac{p}{(r^2 + a^2)^{3/2}}$$

**For r >> a:**

$$\boxed{E_{eq} = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \cdot \frac{p}{r^3}}$$

**Direction:** Opposite to the dipole moment вҶ’ **anti-parallel to pвғ—**

### Critical Comparison вӯҗ

| Property | Axial | Equatorial |
|----------|:-----:|:----------:|
| Formula (r >> a) | 2kp/rВі | kp/rВі |
| Ratio | **E_axial = 2 Г— E_equatorial** | Baseline |
| Direction w.r.t. pвғ— | Same (parallel) | Opposite (anti-parallel) |
| Distance dependence | 1/rВі | 1/rВі |

### Formula 3: Torque on a Dipole in Uniform Field вӯҗвӯҗ

$$\boxed{\vec{\tau} = \vec{p} \times \vec{E}}$$
$$|\tau| = pE\sin\theta$$

Where Оё = angle between pвғ— and Eвғ—.

| Оё | П„ | Condition |
|:-:|:-:|:----------|
| 0В° | 0 | Stable equilibrium (p вҲҘ E) |
| 90В° | pE (maximum) | Maximum torque |
| 180В° | 0 | Unstable equilibrium (p anti-вҲҘ E) |

### Formula 4: Potential Energy of Dipole вӯҗ

$$\boxed{U = -\vec{p} \cdot \vec{E} = -pE\cos\theta}$$

| Оё | U | Stability |
|:-:|:-:|:----------|
| 0В° | вҲ’pE | **Minimum energy вҶ’ stable** |
| 90В° | 0 | Reference |
| 180В° | +pE | **Maximum energy вҶ’ unstable** |

### Formula 5: Work done in rotating a dipole

$$W = U_f - U_i = pE(\cos\theta_1 - \cos\theta_2)$$

Where ОёвӮҒ is the initial angle and ОёвӮӮ is the final angle with Eвғ—.

## рҹ§ұ Stage 3: Type-wise Mastery

### Type 1: Calculate dipole moment вӯҗ

**Solved Example** рҹҹў

> Two charges +5 nC and вҲ’5 nC are separated by 2 mm. Find the dipole moment.

**Solution:**
p = q Г— 2a = 5 Г— 10вҒ»вҒ№ Г— 2 Г— 10вҒ»Ві = **10вҒ»В№В№ CВ·m = 10 pCВ·m**

Direction: from вҲ’5 nC to +5 nC.

**Practice:**

1. рҹҹў Charges Вұ2 ОјC separated by 1 cm. Find p. *(Ans: 2 Г— 10вҒ»вҒё CВ·m)*
2. рҹҹЎ The dipole moment of a water molecule is 6.17 Г— 10вҒ»ВівҒ° CВ·m. The separation between the charges is 0.96 Г…. Find the charge. *(Ans: q = p/2a = 6.43 Г— 10вҒ»ВІвҒ° C вүҲ 0.4e вҖ” showing partial charge separation)*

### Type 2: Axial field of a dipole вӯҗвӯҗ

**Solved Example** рҹҹЎ

> A dipole with moment p = 4 Г— 10вҒ»вҒ№ CВ·m is placed along the x-axis. Find the electric field at a point 20 cm away on the axis.

**Solution:**
E = 2kp/rВі = 2 Г— 9 Г— 10вҒ№ Г— 4 Г— 10вҒ»вҒ№ / (0.2)Ві
= 72 / 0.008 = **9000 N/C = 9 kN/C**

Direction: along the dipole moment (from вҲ’q to +q).

**Practice:**

1. рҹҹў Find E on the axis at 10 cm from a dipole of p = 10вҒ»В№В№ CВ·m. *(Ans: 0.18 N/C)*
2. рҹҹЎ The axial field at distance r is 500 N/C. What is the field at 2r? *(Ans: 500/8 = 62.5 N/C вҖ” since E вҲқ 1/rВі)*
3. рҹҹЎ вӯҗ At what distance on the axis is the field due to a dipole (p = 10вҒ»вҒё CВ·m) equal to 10вҒө N/C? *(Ans: rВі = 2kp/E вҶ’ r вүҲ 0.012 m = 1.2 cm)*

### Type 3: Equatorial field of a dipole вӯҗвӯҗ

**Solved Example** рҹҹЎ

> Using the same dipole from Type 2 (p = 4 Г— 10вҒ»вҒ№ CВ·m), find E at 20 cm on the equatorial line.

**Solution:**
E = kp/rВі = 9 Г— 10вҒ№ Г— 4 Г— 10вҒ»вҒ№ / (0.2)Ві
= 36 / 0.008 = **4500 N/C**

This is exactly **half** the axial field. вң“

Direction: anti-parallel to pвғ—.

**Practice:**

1. рҹҹЎ At a point on the equatorial line, E = 200 N/C. What would E be at the same distance on the axial line? *(Ans: 400 N/C)*
2. рҹҹЎ вӯҗ At distance r on the axis, E = EвӮҖ. At what distance on the equatorial line is E = EвӮҖ? *(Ans: r/2^{1/3} = r Г— 0.794)*

### Type 4: Torque on a dipole вӯҗвӯҗвӯҗ

**Solved Example** рҹҹЎ

> A dipole (p = 3 Г— 10вҒ»вҒё CВ·m) is placed in a uniform electric field of 10вҒө N/C, making an angle of 30В° with the field. Find the torque.

**Solution:**
П„ = pE sin Оё = 3 Г— 10вҒ»вҒё Г— 10вҒө Г— sin 30В°
= 3 Г— 10вҒ»Ві Г— 0.5 = **1.5 Г— 10вҒ»Ві NВ·m**

**Practice:**

1. рҹҹў p = 2 Г— 10вҒ»вҒ№ CВ·m, E = 10вҒҙ N/C, Оё = 90В°. Find П„. *(Ans: 2 Г— 10вҒ»вҒө NВ·m)*
2. рҹҹЎ At what angle is the torque maximum? *(Ans: 90В°)*
3. рҹҹЎ вӯҗ At what angle is the torque half of the maximum? *(Ans: sin Оё = 0.5 вҶ’ Оё = 30В° or 150В°)*
4. рҹ”ҙ A dipole (Вұ10 ОјC, separation 2 cm) is placed in E = 10вҒө N/C at 60В°. Find (a) torque, (b) force on each charge, (c) net force.

<details>
<summary><b>Answer</b></summary>

p = 10 Г— 10вҒ»вҒ¶ Г— 0.02 = 2 Г— 10вҒ»вҒ· CВ·m

(a) П„ = pE sin 60В° = 2 Г— 10вҒ»вҒ· Г— 10вҒө Г— (вҲҡ3/2) = **вҲҡ3 Г— 10вҒ»ВІ NВ·m вүҲ 0.0173 NВ·m**

(b) Force on each charge = qE = 10 Г— 10вҒ»вҒ¶ Г— 10вҒө = **1 N**

(c) In a uniform field, the net force on a dipole = **0** (the forces on +q and вҲ’q are equal and opposite). Only torque exists.
</details>

### Type 5: Potential energy and work done вӯҗвӯҗ

**Solved Example** рҹҹЎ

> A dipole (p = 2 Г— 10вҒ»вҒ¶ CВ·m) initially aligned with a field E = 10вҒҙ N/C is rotated to 90В°. Find the work done.

**Solution:**
W = pE(cos ОёвӮҒ вҲ’ cos ОёвӮӮ) = 2 Г— 10вҒ»вҒ¶ Г— 10вҒҙ Г— (cos 0В° вҲ’ cos 90В°)
= 0.02 Г— (1 вҲ’ 0) = **0.02 J**

**Practice:**

1. рҹҹў Work to rotate from 0В° to 180В°. *(Ans: 2pE)*
2. рҹҹЎ вӯҗ Work to rotate from 60В° to 90В°. *(Ans: pE(cos 60В° вҲ’ cos 90В°) = pE/2)*
3. рҹҹЎ Work to rotate from 90В° to 180В°. *(Ans: pE(cos 90В° вҲ’ cos 180В°) = pE)*
4. рҹ”ҙ вӯҗ A dipole is released from Оё = 60В° in a uniform field. What is its angular speed when it passes through Оё = 0В°? (Moment of inertia = I)

<details>
<summary><b>Answer</b></summary>

By conservation of energy: Loss in PE = Gain in KE

pE(cos 0В° вҲ’ cos 60В°) = ВҪIПүВІ

pE(1 вҲ’ 0.5) = ВҪIПүВІ

**Пү = вҲҡ(pE/I)**
</details>

### Type 6: Net force on a dipole in a non-uniform field

**Key insight:** In a **uniform** field, net force on a dipole = 0 (only torque). In a **non-uniform** field, there IS a net force.

**Solved Example** рҹ”ҙ

> A dipole (p directed along +x) is placed in a non-uniform field E = EвӮҖ(x/a) xМӮ. The centre of the dipole is at x = xвӮҖ. Find the net force.

**Solution:**

Force on +q at xвӮҖ + a: FвӮҠ = q Г— EвӮҖ(xвӮҖ + a)/a, in +x
Force on вҲ’q at xвӮҖ вҲ’ a: FвӮӢ = вҲ’q Г— EвӮҖ(xвӮҖ вҲ’ a)/a, in +x

Net force = qEвӮҖ[(xвӮҖ + a) вҲ’ (xвӮҖ вҲ’ a)]/a = qEвӮҖ(2a)/a = **2qEвӮҖ = pEвӮҖ/a**

More generally: **F = p(dE/dx)** for a dipole in a non-uniform field.

**Practice:**

1. рҹ”ҙ A dipole is near a positive point charge Q at distance r (r >> a). Is the dipole attracted or repelled? *(Ans: attracted вҖ” because the nearer charge of the dipole is opposite to Q and experiences stronger force than the farther same-sign charge)*

### Type 7: Derivation вҖ” Field on the axial line вӯҗвӯҗ (Board must-know)

**This derivation is asked almost every year. Memorize the logic.**

**Setup:** Dipole with вҲ’q at A and +q at B, separated by 2a. Point P is on the axis at distance r from center O.

**E at P due to +q:** EвӮҠ = kq/(r вҲ’ a)ВІ (directed away from +q, i.e., +x)
**E at P due to вҲ’q:** EвӮӢ = kq/(r + a)ВІ (directed toward вҲ’q, i.e., вҲ’x)

**Net E = EвӮҠ вҲ’ EвӮӢ** (since EвӮҠ > EвӮӢ, net is along +x = direction of p)

$$E = kq\left[\frac{1}{(r-a)^2} - \frac{1}{(r+a)^2}\right] = kq \cdot \frac{4ar}{(r^2-a^2)^2}$$

Since p = q(2a):

$$E_{axial} = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \cdot \frac{2pr}{(r^2-a^2)^2}$$

For r >> a: (rВІ вҲ’ aВІ)ВІ вүҲ rвҒҙ

$$\boxed{E_{axial} \approx \frac{2kp}{r^3}}$$

---

### Type 8: Derivation вҖ” Field on the equatorial line вӯҗвӯҗ (Board must-know)

**Setup:** Point P is at distance r from center O, on the perpendicular bisector.

Distance from each charge to P: вҲҡ(rВІ + aВІ)

EвӮҠ and EвӮӢ have equal magnitudes: E = kq/(rВІ + aВІ)

By symmetry, **vertical components cancel**. Only horizontal (вҲ’x direction) survives.

Each horizontal component: E cos Оё = E Г— a/вҲҡ(rВІ + aВІ)

$$E_{eq} = 2 \times \frac{kq}{r^2+a^2} \times \frac{a}{\sqrt{r^2+a^2}} = \frac{kq \times 2a}{(r^2+a^2)^{3/2}} = \frac{kp}{(r^2+a^2)^{3/2}}$$

For r >> a:

$$\boxed{E_{eq} \approx \frac{kp}{r^3}}$$

Direction: **opposite to pвғ—** (вҲ’x direction = from +q to вҲ’q).

## рҹ”Җ Stage 4: Type Mixer

**Q1.** рҹ”ҙ вӯҗ A dipole is placed in a uniform field E. The dipole is initially at Оё = 90В°. Find: (a) torque, (b) PE, (c) work to bring it to 0В°, (d) work to bring it to 180В°.

<details>
<summary><b>Solution</b></summary>

(a) П„ = pE sin 90В° = **pE**
(b) U = вҲ’pE cos 90В° = **0**
(c) W(90В° вҶ’ 0В°) = pE(cos 90В° вҲ’ cos 0В°) = pE(0 вҲ’ 1) = **вҲ’pE** (energy released)
(d) W(90В° вҶ’ 180В°) = pE(cos 90В° вҲ’ cos 180В°) = pE(0 вҲ’ (вҲ’1)) = **+pE** (energy required)
</details>

**Q2.** рҹ”ҙ вӯҗ At a point on the axial line of a short dipole, E = 800 N/C. At the same distance on the equatorial line, E = ?

<details>
<summary><b>Solution</b></summary>

E_axial = 2kp/rВі = 800 N/C
E_equatorial = kp/rВі = 800/2 = **400 N/C**
</details>

**Q3.** рҹ”ҙ A dipole (p = 10вҒ»вҒ· CВ·m) is placed in E = 3 Г— 10вҒҙ N/C at Оё = 30В°. Find (a) torque, (b) PE, (c) work to rotate to 60В°.

<details>
<summary><b>Solution</b></summary>

(a) П„ = pE sin 30В° = 10вҒ»вҒ· Г— 3 Г— 10вҒҙ Г— 0.5 = **1.5 Г— 10вҒ»Ві NВ·m**

(b) U = вҲ’pE cos 30В° = вҲ’10вҒ»вҒ· Г— 3 Г— 10вҒҙ Г— (вҲҡ3/2) = **вҲ’2.6 Г— 10вҒ»Ві J**

(c) W = pE(cos 30В° вҲ’ cos 60В°) = 3 Г— 10вҒ»Ві (вҲҡ3/2 вҲ’ 1/2) = 3 Г— 10вҒ»Ві Г— 0.366 = **1.1 Г— 10вҒ»Ві J**
</details>

## рҹ“Ӣ Stage 5: Board Arsenal

**Q1.** рҹҹЎ вӯҗ Define electric dipole and electric dipole moment. *(2 marks)*

<details>
<summary><b>Model Answer</b></summary>

**Electric dipole:** A system of two equal and opposite charges (+q and вҲ’q) separated by a small fixed distance (2a) is called an electric dipole.

**Electric dipole moment (pвғ—):** It is defined as the product of the magnitude of either charge and the distance between the charges: p = q Г— 2a. It is a vector directed from the negative charge to the positive charge. SI unit: CВ·m.
</details>

**Q2.** рҹ”ҙ вӯҗ Derive the expression for the electric field at a point on the axial line of an electric dipole. *(5 marks)*

<details>
<summary><b>Model Answer</b></summary>

*(See Type 7 above for the complete derivation. Write it step-by-step with a diagram.)*

**Key result:** E_axial = (1/4ПҖОөвӮҖ) Г— 2pr/(rВІвҲ’aВІ)ВІ вүҲ (1/4ПҖОөвӮҖ) Г— 2p/rВі for r >> a

Direction: along the dipole moment vector.
</details>

**Q3.** рҹ”ҙ вӯҗ Derive the expression for torque on an electric dipole placed in a uniform electric field. *(5 marks)*

<details>
<summary><b>Model Answer</b></summary>

Consider a dipole (charges +q and вҲ’q, separation 2a) making angle Оё with uniform field E.

Force on +q: FвӮҠ = qE (along E)
Force on вҲ’q: FвӮӢ = qE (opposite to E)

Net force = 0 (uniform field).

These equal, opposite, non-collinear forces form a **couple**.

Torque = force Г— perpendicular distance between lines of action
= qE Г— 2a sin Оё = pE sin Оё

In vector form: **П„вғ— = pвғ— Г— Eвғ—**

The torque tends to align the dipole along the field (Оё вҶ’ 0).
</details>

**Q4.** рҹҹЎ вӯҗ An electric dipole is placed in a uniform electric field. When is (a) torque maximum, (b) torque minimum, (c) PE maximum, (d) PE minimum? *(3 marks)*

<details>
<summary><b>Model Answer</b></summary>

(a) Torque maximum when Оё = 90В° (p вҠҘ E): П„_max = pE
(b) Torque minimum (zero) when Оё = 0В° or 180В° (p вҲҘ E or p anti-вҲҘ E)
(c) PE maximum when Оё = 180В° (p anti-parallel to E): U = +pE (unstable equilibrium)
(d) PE minimum when Оё = 0В° (p parallel to E): U = вҲ’pE (stable equilibrium)
</details>

## рҹҡҖ Stage 6: JEE Mains Arena

**Q1.** рҹҹЎ вӯҗ The ratio of the electric field due to a short dipole on its axial line to that on its equatorial line at the same distance is:

(a) 1:1 &emsp; (b) 2:1 &emsp; (c) 1:2 &emsp; (d) 4:1

<details>
<summary><b>Answer</b></summary>

E_axial/E_eq = (2kp/rВі)/(kp/rВі) = **2:1**

**Answer: (b)**
</details>

**Q2.** рҹ”ҙ вӯҗ An electric dipole is placed at an angle of 30В° with a uniform electric field of 10вҒҙ N/C. It experiences a torque of 5 NВ·m. The charge on the dipole, if the dipole length is 2 cm, is:

(a) 5 mC &emsp; (b) 50 mC &emsp; (c) 500 mC &emsp; (d) 7 ОјC

<details>
<summary><b>Answer</b></summary>

П„ = pE sin Оё вҶ’ p = П„/(E sin Оё) = 5/(10вҒҙ Г— 0.5) = 10вҒ»Ві CВ·m

p = q Г— 2a вҶ’ q = p/(2a) = 10вҒ»Ві/0.02 = 0.05 C = **50 mC**

**Answer: (b)**
</details>

**Q3.** рҹ”ҙ вӯҗ The work done in rotating a dipole from stable equilibrium (Оё = 0В°) to Оё = 60В° in a uniform field is W. The work to rotate from 60В° to 90В° is:

(a) W &emsp; (b) W/2 &emsp; (c) (вҲҡ3 вҲ’ 1)W &emsp; (d) W(2 вҲ’ вҲҡ3)/(2 вҲ’ вҲҡ3)

<details>
<summary><b>Answer</b></summary>

WвӮҒ (0В° вҶ’ 60В°) = pE(cos 0В° вҲ’ cos 60В°) = pE(1 вҲ’ 1/2) = pE/2 = W
вҶ’ pE = 2W

WвӮӮ (60В° вҶ’ 90В°) = pE(cos 60В° вҲ’ cos 90В°) = 2W(1/2 вҲ’ 0) = **W**

**Answer: (a)**

> рҹ”‘ Trap alert: Many students think the second rotation requires more work because the angle is "bigger." But W depends on the change in cos Оё, not the change in Оё.
</details>

**Q4.** рҹ”ҙ A dipole of moment p is placed perpendicular to a uniform field E. The work done in rotating it to the direction of the field is:

(a) pE &emsp; (b) вҲ’pE &emsp; (c) 2pE &emsp; (d) вҲ’2pE

<details>
<summary><b>Answer</b></summary>

W = pE(cos 90В° вҲ’ cos 0В°) = pE(0 вҲ’ 1) = **вҲ’pE**

The negative sign means **energy is released** (the dipole naturally wants to align, so the field does positive work = pE, and work done against the field = вҲ’pE).

**Answer: (b)**
</details>

**Q5.** рҹ”ҙ An electric dipole is free to move in a non-uniform electric field. It will:

(a) Move toward stronger field  
(b) Move toward weaker field  
(c) Not move at all  
(d) Rotate only  

<details>
<summary><b>Answer</b></summary>

**(a)** вҖ” In a non-uniform field, the force on the closer charge is stronger. After the dipole aligns with the field, it moves toward the region of stronger field (the force on the nearer charge dominates).
</details>
