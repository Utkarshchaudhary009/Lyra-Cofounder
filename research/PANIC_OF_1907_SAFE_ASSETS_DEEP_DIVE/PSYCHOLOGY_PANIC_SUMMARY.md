# Why Smart People Became Fools in 1907: Psychology & Neuroscience
## A Living Summary of Key Psychological Findings

---

## TL;DR (Too Long; Didn't Read)

**Question:** Why did intelligent bankers, businesspeople, and investors not see the Panic of 1907 coming?

**Answer:** Their brains were literally unable to think rationally when they needed it most.

### The Core Problem:

During boom times (1897-1906):
- People used **fast, intuitive thinking** (System 1)
- They made logical-sounding arguments for crazy risks
- They thought they were geniuses

When crisis hit (October 1907):
- Their **fear system** (amygdala) activated
- Their **rational system** (prefrontal cortex) shut down
- They could only feel panic, not think clearly

---

## The Two Brain Systems (Kahneman)

### System 1: Fast Brain
- Automatic, intuitive
- Makes ~35,000 decisions/day
- Uses mental shortcuts (heuristics)
- TAKES OVER during stress

**In 1907 Boom:** "Growth forever!" ✓ System 1 Dominant
**In 1907 Crisis:** "PANIC! RUN!" ✓ System 1 Dominant

### System 2: Slow Brain
- Analytical, rational
- Takes time and energy
- Weighs evidence carefully
- **Gets completely overwhelmed during crisis**

**In 1907 Crisis:** "Wait, let me think about this..." ✗ System 2 OFFLINE

## The Five Cognitive Biases That Caused 1907

### 1. OVERCONFIDENCE BIAS (Built the Bomb)

**What happened:**
- 1897-1906: Economy boomed massively
- Smart people thought: "I understand this. I'm genius."
- They built elaborate logical arguments for insane leverage
- "Historical growth is 7%. Therefore 30% returns are justified."
- "The economy will always grow. Therefore debt is safe."

**Why it's worse for smart people:**
- Dumb people: "Market up. I buy."
- Smart people: "Let me construct a logical proof that leverage is optimal..."
- Smart people can RATIONALIZE any belief with enough logic

**Result:** Massive overleveraging, hidden risks everywhere

---

### 2. CONFIRMATION BIAS (Masked the Warning Signs)

**What happened:**
- Summer 1906: Copper prices crash 40%
- Banker thinks: "Just one sector. Economy is fine."
- Ignores: Copper is a leading economic indicator
- Ignores: Bank of England raising rates for a reason

**Why it's worse for smart people:**
- When you WANT something to be true, your brain FILTERS OUT contradictions
- Smart people are better at constructing arguments to ignore evidence
- "That data doesn't apply to us because [elaborate reason]"

**Result:** People didn't see danger signs that were right in front of them

---

### 3. ANCHORING BIAS (Kept People Trapped)

**What happened:**
- Depositor had $1,000 in "safe" bank
- Last year: Bank was solid
- Anchor: $1,000 = Safe
- This month: Bank losing deposits fast
- New information: Danger!
- Brain response: "No, it's an ANCHOR at safe. Must be okay."

**For investors:**
- Bought stock at $100
- Anchor: "I paid $100"
- Stock falls to $30
- Think: "It'll get back to $100 someday"
- Hold and lose more money

**Result:** Bad decisions based on old, irrelevant information

---

### 4. INFORMATION CASCADES (Spread the Panic)

**The Cascade Effect:**

```
October 16: Two speculators fail (REAL PROBLEM)
    ↓
October 18: People start withdrawing (RATIONAL)
    ↓
October 20: Rumors spread (NOBODY KNOWS FACTS)
    ↓
October 22: EVERYONE withdraws from even SAFE banks
    ↓
October 24: Safe banks ACTUALLY FAIL (RUMORS MADE IT TRUE)
```

**How cascades work:**
1. Person A withdraws (maybe for real reason)
2. Person B sees A withdrawing
3. B thinks: "If A is withdrawing, A knows something I don't"
4. B withdraws
5. Person C sees A & B: "Must be a real problem!"
6. C withdraws
7. **EVERYONE acts on rumors as if they were facts**
8. **Rumors become self-fulfilling prophecies**

**The key finding from research:**
Rumors CAUSED bank failures, not just revealed them.

Example: Trust Company X might have been solvent. But rumors made people withdraw. The withdrawals made them insolvent. The rumors became TRUE.

---

### 5. LOSS AVERSION (Triggered the Panic)

**The principle:**
People feel losses **2x more intensely** than gains of the same size.

Gain $100: "Good."
Lose $100: "TERRIBLE" (2x the emotional intensity)

**In 1907:**
- Possible loss of deposits: **MAXIMUM LOSS AVERSION ACTIVATION**
- Potential loss > everything else
- "GET MONEY OUT NOW!" (before losing it all)
- "Think about it rationally later" (never happens)

**Result:** Rational people making seemingly-irrational panicked decisions
(But from an evolutionary perspective: During saber-tooth tiger attacks, you RAN first, thought later.)

## The Neuroscience: The Amygdala Hijack

### What the Amygdala Does:
- Detects threats
- Triggers "fight or flight"
- Releases cortisol (stress hormone)
- Releases adrenaline
- **SHUTS DOWN the prefrontal cortex** (rational thinking)

### The Brain Sequence in Crisis:

```
TRIGGER: News that Heinze/Morse failed

AMYGDALA: "THREAT! MONEY AT RISK!"

STRESS HORMONES: Cortisol floods system

PREFRONTAL CORTEX: Shuts down

RESULT: Person runs to bank, withdraws money

AFTERMATH: "I'll analyze this rationally later" (But the panic is over by then)
```

### Scientific Evidence:

**Study 1: Stress Makes You WORSE at Financial Decisions**
- Researchers induced acute stress (ice water task)
- People making investment decisions under stress
- Results: Made MORE biased choices, LESS rational
- Under stress: Took more risk on losses, less on gains
- **The reflection effect DOUBLED under stress**

**Study 2: Fear Impairs Information Processing**
- Fearful people made worse decisions when given bad news
- Brain too busy processing fear to process information
- Some people became "contrarians" - HUGE risky bets to counteract fear
- "I'll take insane risks to feel better"

**Study 3: Financial Stress Physically Changes Brain Structure**
- People experiencing financial hardship had SMALLER amygdala and hippocampus
- Financial stress literally shrinks the brain regions needed for rational thinking
- This makes it even HARDER to think rationally next time

### Implication for 1907:

As panic spread:
1. People got scared
2. Fear made them make worse decisions
3. Worse decisions caused more panic
4. Panic made them more scared
5. **VICIOUS CYCLE**

## Why Intelligent People Were MOST Vulnerable

### The Paradox:

**Smart people sometimes make the WORST financial decisions.**

### Why?

**Smart people can build elaborate logical arguments for wrong beliefs.**

**Example:**

Unintelligent person: "Market going up. I buy stocks."
- Simple, maybe lucky

Smart person: "Let me construct a logical argument..."
- Historical growth rates: 7% annual
- Innovation is accelerating
- Railroads transform economy
- Leverage amplifies gains
- Therefore: 30% returns justified
- Anyone NOT leveraging is irrational
- I'm intelligent, so I see what others miss

**The Problem:**
Smart person's argument SOUNDS logical. But it's built on FALSE PREMISES.

When crisis hit, they couldn't escape their own logic cage.

---

## The Three Deadly Biases for Intelligent People

### 1. Overconfidence
- "I'm smart therefore I understand the market"
- "Intelligence = foresight"
- Mistaking cleverness for wisdom

### 2. Confirmation Bias (MOST DANGEROUS for smart people)
- Smart people can construct elaborate justifications for ignoring evidence
- They're better at rationalization
- "That data doesn't apply because [1,000 words of logic]"

### 3. Authority Bias
- "Other smart people must know something I don't"
- "If JP Morgan thinks it's safe, it must be"
- Not realizing Morgan also works with imperfect info

## Why The Panic Pattern Repeats EVERY TIME

### The Universal Crisis Sequence:

**Phase 1: Overconfidence Boom**
- Everyone thinks crisis can't happen
- Smart people especially confident
- Leverage increases dramatically

**Phase 2: Shock/Trigger**
- Something REAL happens (earthquake, bankruptcy, surprise event)
- Amygdala activates across entire population
- Fear spreads

**Phase 3: Information Cascade**
- Rumors spread faster than facts
- People withdraw from "uncertain" institutions
- Even solvent institutions fail from runs
- Rumors become self-fulfilling prophecies

**Phase 4: Panic Cycle (Vicious)**
- Fear → Worse decisions → More losses → More fear
- Each person's panic triggers others' panic
- Downward spiral

**Phase 5: Recovery When:**
- Authority figure acts confident (JP Morgan showed up with cash)
- New information appears (official reports)
- Structural changes stop the runs (deposit insurance, central bank)

### This Pattern Repeated In:
- 1893 Panic (US)
- **1907 Panic (US)**
- 1929 Great Depression
- 1987 Black Monday
- 1998 LTCM Crisis
- **2008 Financial Crisis**
- **2020 COVID Crash** (brief - stopped quickly because circuit breakers worked)

**The pattern is so consistent we can model it mathematically.**

## What This Teaches About Human Nature

### The Good News:
✓ We understand the psychology and neuroscience now
✓ Every bias is documented and measurable
✓ We can predict when panics will happen

### The Bad News:
✗ Understanding the bias doesn't make you immune
✗ Knowing about loss aversion doesn't eliminate it
✗ Understanding the amygdala doesn't disable it in crisis
✗ You CANNOT overcome these biases through willpower alone

### The Real Solution:

**Don't rely on individual rationality. Build systems that work FOR the irrational brain.**

Systems that worked:
1. **Deposit Insurance** - Stops bank runs regardless of fear
2. **Lender of Last Resort** - Federal Reserve provides unlimited liquidity
3. **Circuit Breakers** - Automatic trading halts
4. **Transparent Information** - Required disclosure rules
5. **Regulation** - Prevents dangerous overleveraging

These systems are BRILLIANT because:
- They don't assume people are rational
- They work BY preventing panic
- They align with human nature, not against it

## Key Takeaways

### About the 1907 Panic:
1. Smart people WEREN'T stupid - their brains were optimized for survival, not markets
2. Overconfidence was rational during the boom (you couldn't predict the shock)
3. Panic was rational during the crisis (you can't afford to guess with your deposits)
4. Information cascades made rumors more powerful than facts
5. JP Morgan stopped the panic not by argument, but by providing confidence and cash

### About Financial Crises in General:
1. They follow a predictable psychological pattern
2. The pattern repeats because human brains don't change
3. You can't prevent them through individual rationality
4. You CAN prevent them through good systems

### About You:
1. You will make biased financial decisions
2. Knowing about the bias doesn't help much
3. Systems (automatic investments, diversification, insurance) help more than willpower
4. Fear is not a character flaw - it's ancient brain software trying to save you
