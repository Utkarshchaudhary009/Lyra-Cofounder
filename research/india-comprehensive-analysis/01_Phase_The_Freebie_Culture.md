
## 6. Case Studies: The Collapse of State Machinery
Specific examples of how "free" promises destroyed operational viability.

### A. The Power Crisis (Punjab & Tamil Nadu)
*   **The Promise:** Free electricity for farmers and households.
*   **The Reality:**
    *   **Punjab (PSPCL):** In FY24, the state owed its own power corporation ₹7,000 crore in unpaid subsidies. Result: PSPCL posted a loss of ₹4,500 crore.
    *   **Tamil Nadu (TANGEDCO):** The state had to inject ~₹31,850 crore just to keep the lights on. Despite this, TANGEDCO is bleeding, and AT&C losses (theft/leakage) rose to ~12.9%.
    *   **Impact:** Instead of upgrading transformers or lines (Capex), money is used to plug the gap of "free power."

### B. The Transport Crisis (Karnataka "Shakti" Scheme)
*   **The Promise:** Free bus travel for women.
*   **The Reality:**
    *   **Ridership:** Up by 23%, but revenue didn't follow.
    *   **The Debt:** The Govt owes KSRTC ₹2,182 crore in reimbursements (March 2025).
    *   **Consequence:** KSRTC cannot buy new buses or maintain old ones efficiently.
    *   **Irony:** To cover losses, they raised fares for *men* and other non-free passengers by 15%. A tax on the working class to fund the freebie.

## 7. The Inflation Connection (Cash vs. Production)
*   **Mechanism:** When you give cash (Unconditional Transfers) without increasing the supply of goods (Production), prices rise.
*   **Data:** Cash transfers have grown 23-fold in 10 years (₹2.8 lakh crore).
*   **Impact:** This adds ~1.5% to consumption demand. Since rural supply chains (cold storage, logistics) weren't built (because Capex was cut), food prices spiked.
*   **Result:** The poor get ₹1000 free, but inflation eats ₹1200.

## 8. Final Synthesis: The Vicious Cycle
1.  **Freebie Issued:** Government gives free power/cash.
2.  **State Bankruptcy:** Treasury runs out of money for Capex.
3.  **Infrastructure Decay:** No new roads, power grids, or cold storage.
4.  **Supply Shocks:** Poor infrastructure causes food wastage -> Inflation.
5.  **Real Income Drop:** Inflation erodes the value of the freebie.
6.  **Demand for MORE Freebies:** The public demands higher handouts to survive inflation.
7.  **Cycle Intensifies:** The trap closes.
