## 1. The Statistical Mirage: Per Capita vs. Real Growth
*   **The Number:** India's per capita income is projected to reach ~$3,000 by 2026.
*   **The Reality:** Growth averaged ~6.5% over the decade, but it was interrupted by a -6.5% crash in 2020.
*   **The Divide:** While the average income rises, the **median income** (the income of the person in the middle) remains stuck at ~₹10,000/month ($120).

## 2. Rural Wage Stagnation (The 2015-2023 Lost Decade)
*   **Data:** Between 2014 and 2023, real wages for agricultural workers grew by **less than 1%** annually.
*   **Inflation Impact:** During the 2021-23 global shocks, rural inflation (food) outpaced wage growth.
*   **Recent Resurgence:** FY25 saw a bounce-back with ~4-7% growth, but it follows a decade of near-zero real gains.

## 3. The "K-Shaped" Recovery (The Billionaire Raj)
*   **Concentration:** The top 1% now hold **40.1% of national wealth** (historical high).
*   **The Gap:** The top 10% receive 58% of income, while the bottom 50% get only 15%.
*   **Conflict:** SBI reports claim inequality is falling based on "taxable income," but global bodies (World Inequality Lab) confirm that the actual wealth gap is widening rapidly.

## 4. The 15-Year IT Salary Freeze
*   **The Stagnation:** In 2010, a TCS/Infosys fresher earned ~₹3.2 LPA. In 2025, many still start at ~₹3.5-4 LPA.
*   **Inflation Adjustment:** If salaries had tracked inflation, an entry-level IT job should pay ₹10-12 LPA today.
*   **CEO vs. Worker:** CEO salaries rose 1500% in 10 years, while freshers' salaries rose only 46%.
*   **The Shift:** Companies only pay high for "Specialized" roles (AI/ML), leaving the average engineer in a low-income trap.

## 5. The Gig Economy: The New "Working Poor"
*   **Earnings:** A Swiggy/Zomato worker earns ~₹102/hour gross.
*   **Net Income:** After fuel, maintenance, and unpaid waiting time, they take home ₹15,000-22,000/month.
*   **Real Decline:** Real wages in the gig sector fell by 11% between 2019 and 2022.
*   **Conditions:** Most work 60-70 hours a week with zero social security or insurance.

## 6. The "Babu" vs. Private Divide
*   **Public Sector Premium:** Government jobs pay **62% to 102% more** than equivalent private-sector formal jobs.
*   **The 7th Pay Commission:** Raised minimum basic pay from ₹7,440 to ₹18,000 (142% jump).
*   **The Result:** This disparity drives the "Govt Job Obsession" (Phase 2), as the private sector provides no comparable security or wage growth.

## 7. The GST & MSME Squeeze
*   **Compliance Burden:** MSMEs face annual compliance costs of ₹13-17 lakh, navigating 1,400 rules.
*   **Digital Divide:** Micro-enterprises lack the tech to file GST, leading to consultants' fees and late penalties.
*   **Squeeze:** Many small businesses have seen profit margins shrink as they cannot pass on GST costs to consumers.

## 8. Synthesis: The "Low Income Trap" Mechanism
1.  **Inflation Evasion:** Costs of food/fuel rise faster than wages.
2.  **Productivity Gap:** Skills are low (Phase 2), so companies don't raise wages.
3.  **Monopoly Power:** Large corps consolidate, reducing the bargaining power of workers and small suppliers.
4.  **No Savings:** Low income leads to zero household savings (lowest in decades).
5.  **No Investment:** With no savings, families can't invest in assets (Phase 9) or better education, ensuring the next generation stays in the same trap.
