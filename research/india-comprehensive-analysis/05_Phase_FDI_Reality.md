## 1. The FDI Paradox: Gross Inflow vs. Net Realit
*   **The Trend:** India's Gross FDI inflow reached a record provisional ~$81 billion in FY25.
*   **The Reality:** **Net FDI** (the actual money staying in India) crashed to a record low of ~$0.4 - $1 billion.
*   **The Reason:** Foreign investors pulled out ~$49 billion in profits and disinvestments. While new money is coming in, "old money" is leaving faster than ever before.
*   **The Message:** Global investors view India as a place to make a quick profit and exit, rather than a place for long-term "deep" asset building.

## 2. The Great Corporate Exit (2020-2025)
*   **Automotive:** Ford and Harley-Davidson exited manufacturing after losing billions. Reasons: Unsuitable product strategy and inability to compete with low-margin local players.
*   **Cement:** Holcim (Ambuja/ACC) sold its entire Indian business to Adani.
*   **Common Themes:** High losses, regulatory hurdles, and a pivot to "greener" or more predictable markets elsewhere.

## 3. Services vs. Manufacturing
*   **Dependency:** Services (IT, Software, Finance) still attract the lion's share (~35% of total equity).
*   **Manufacturing Lag:** Despite PLI schemes, Manufacturing FDI is only ~$19 billion (23%).
*   **PLI Success:** Working well for Mobile Phones (Apple/Foxconn) and Pharma, but yet to create a broad "industrial revolution."

## 4. Retrospective Tax & Policy Uncertainty
*   **Legacy Issues:** The Vodafone and Cairn cases ($1.4B award) created a "Tax Terror" image that lingers.
*   **Stability:** Although the law was scrapped in 2021, recent shifts (Mauritius treaty tweaks) keep investors on edge. Predictability is still the #1 demand of global CFOs.

## 5. Ease of Enforcing Contracts (Rank 163)
*   **The Black Hole:** It takes an average of **4 years** to resolve a commercial dispute in India.
*   **Global Rank:** India was ranked 163 out of 190 (lower than many African nations) before the World Bank discontinued the report.
*   **FDI Impact:** Manufacturers hesitate to set up large plants if they know a supplier dispute could freeze their capital for half a decade.

## 6. The Vietnam Comparison
*   **Mobile Exports:** India reached ~$30B in 2025, rapidly closing the gap with Vietnam (~$54B).
*   **The Edge:** Vietnam still leads in "cluster manufacturing." Samsung produces **60% of its global phones** in Vietnam.
*   **Protectionism:** India's high import tariffs on components (18% duty) often make it cheaper to assemble in Vietnam than in India for global exports.

## 7. Arbitration Awards: A Trust Deficit
*   **Government Behavior:** In cases like Devas-Antrix, the Indian government has used "state immunity" and curative jurisdiction to challenge and set aside international awards.
*   **FDI Sentiment:** This sends a message that the Indian State is a "sore loser" in legal battles, discouraging investors who fear dealing with government entities.

## 8. Data Localization: The New Tech Hurdle
*   **Compliance:** The DPDPA 2023 and RBI norms require data to be stored *physically* in India.
*   **Cost:** This adds millions in infrastructure costs for global cloud and payment firms (Google, Visa, Mastercard).
*   **Risk:** Smaller tech firms may simply choose not to enter the Indian market due to these heavy compliance burdens.

## 9. Synthesis: Why Foreign Money Hesitates
1.  **High Gross, Low Net:** Investors are exiting as fast as they are entering.
2.  **Legal Labyrinth:** 4-year wait for justice is a "no-go" for global manufacturing.
3.  **Tariff Terror:** High duties on inputs (18%) contradict the "Make in India" goal for global exports.
4.  **Policy Shocks:** Sudden regulatory changes (data, tax, treaties) create a "risk premium" on Indian assets.
5.  **Result:** FDI stays in "Low-Asset" sectors like Services (easy to exit) rather than "High-Asset" sectors like heavy industry (hard to exit).
