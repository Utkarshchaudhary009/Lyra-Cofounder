## 1. The PSU Bank "Cleaning" Scandal
*   **The Number:** Indian banks wrote off **₹16.35 lakh crore** ($200B+) in bad loans between 2014 and 2024.
*   **The Beneficiaries:** 48% of these write-offs were for **Large Industries and Services**.
*   **The Recovery:** Only **14%** of written-off money has been recovered.
*   **Economic Impact:** Taxpayers are effectively subsidizing the failed bets of large corporate houses, while small businesses (MSMEs) struggle for credit.

## 2. Election Spending & Inflation
*   **The Surge:** 2024 was the most expensive election in history (~$14 billion).
*   **Liquidity Spike:** Currency in circulation spiked by **₹89,080 crore** during the polls.
*   **The Price:** This massive injection of cash, combined with pre-election freebies (gas/food subsidies), pushed food inflation to ~5.4% in early 2024.

## 3. The "Raid Raj" Culture
*   **Frequency:** The Enforcement Directorate (ED) initiated **775 cases in FY25**, attaching ₹30,000 crore in assets.
*   **Political Nexus:** Raids are frequently timed around elections or target opposition-linked business houses (e.g., Anil Ambani premises, state-specific crackdowns).
*   **Investor Sentiment:** While some call it "cleaning up," international observers (U.S. Dept of State) note that "media trials" and "abuse of power" erode faith in the rule of law.

## 4. Angel Tax: The Innovation Killer
*   **History:** For a decade, Indian startups faced "Tax Terrorism" where investments were taxed as "income."
*   **The Exodus:** This forced over 30% of Indian tech startups to register in **Dubai or Singapore**.
*   **Correction:** The tax was abolished in 2024, but the trust deficit remains, and the "Brain Drain" of founders continues.

## 5. Crony Capitalism & Wealth Concentration
*   **Rank:** India ranked **10th globally** in the Crony Capitalism Index.
*   **GDP Share:** Wealth from crony sectors (Defense, Construction, Banking) rose from **5% to 8% of GDP**.
*   **The Result:** The top 1% now control **40% of the nation's wealth**, the highest since the British Raj.

## 6. Bureaucracy vs. Farmer Income
*   **Export Bans:** To keep urban food prices low, the government frequently bans exports of Wheat, Rice, and Onions.
*   **Farmer Loss:** Onion farmers lost billions in 2024 as domestic prices crashed while global prices (Pakistan/China) stayed high.
*   **Supply Volatility:** Inconsistent policies (ban today, lift tomorrow) make Indian farmers unreliable for global trade.

## 7. Price Caps & The API Vulnerability
*   **Caps:** The NPPA strictly controls prices of essential drugs.
*   **Dependency:** India still imports **43% of its Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients (APIs)** from China.
*   **Manufacturing Stress:** Small pharma units are shutting down because they cannot survive the double whammy of high Chinese input costs and low government-mandated selling prices.

## 8. RBI: The Golden Goose
*   **Surplus Transfer:** The RBI transferred a record **₹2.69 trillion** to the government in FY25.
*   **Purpose:** This money is used to bridge the budget deficit caused by welfare handouts, effectively using the central bank's reserves to fund "Freebie Politics" (Phase 1).

## 9. Synthesis: How Politics Kills Efficiency
1.  **Arbitrary Rules:** Export bans and price caps destroy market signals.
2.  **Institutional Capture:** RBI and Banks are used as "piggy banks" for political goals.
3.  **Weaponized Agencies:** ED/CBI raids create a climate of fear, not competition.
4.  **The Asset Void:** Capital flees to Singapore/Dubai because the "Babu" (bureaucrat) has too much power over the "Businessman."
