## 1. The "Fake Clean" Scandal: Evergreening
*   **The Concept:** "Evergreening" is when a bank gives a new loan to a failing company just so the company can pay back the old loan. This makes the bank look "Clean" on paper, but the debt is actually growing.
*   **The Warning:** RBI Governor Shaktikanta Das has warned banks against using "Innovative Methods" (like hidden funds) to mask bad loans.
*   **The Reality:** While big corporate loans look okay, micro-loans (for the poor) are failing fast—defaults in micro-finance have **tripled** in one year.

## 2. The 41% Consumption Debt
*   **The Bubble:** Household debt has reached **41.3% of GDP**.
*   **The Risk:** Almost half of this debt (46%) is for "Buying Stuff" (Credit cards, personal loans) rather than "Building Wealth" (Home loans).
*   **The defaults:** Credit card defaults jumped by **28% in 2024**. Indians are borrowing money just to maintain their lifestyle because their salaries are stagnant (Phase 3).

## 3. The Rupee's Slow Slide
*   **The History:** The Rupee was ₹63 per Dollar in 2015. It is now **₹92 in 2026**.
*   **The Reason:** Foreign investors are pulling billions of dollars out of India (~$18 Billion in 2025). 
*   **The Impact:** A weak Rupee makes Petrol and Gold (Phase 9) more expensive for everyone, fueling inflation.

## 4. The Shallow Bond Market
*   **The Problem:** India's corporate bond market is only **15% of GDP** (South Korea is 80%).
*   **The Consequence:** Only the massive companies (Adani/Reliance) can get cheap money from bonds. Small businesses and new startups are forced to go to banks or moneylenders at **24% interest**. The "Financial Pipe" is broken for everyone except the rich.

## 5. The Crypto Exodus
*   **The Talent:** India has the 3rd largest group of Web3 developers in the world.
*   **The Flight:** Because of a **50% tax** and confusing rules, **60% of Indian crypto startups** have moved to Dubai or Singapore. 
*   **The Loss:** India raised the talent, but Dubai gets the taxes and the high-tech jobs.

## 6. The "Gender Gap" in Money
*   **The Stats:** Only **33% of Indian women** understand basic finance. In villages, it's less than 20%.
*   **The Danger:** This makes millions of women easy targets for "Loan Sharks" and high-interest micro-finance traps.

## 7. LIC: The Conglomerate Safety Net
*   **The Exposure:** LIC (where millions of Indians keep their life savings) has invested **₹38,658 crore** in the Adani Group.
*   **The Risk:** LIC is also being used to "Save" failing banks like Yes Bank. If these big companies or banks ever fail, the common man's insurance money will be at risk.

## 8. Synthesis: A Fragile Financial House
1.  **Retail Debt:** The common man is drowning in credit card debt.
2.  **Corporate Masking:** Banks are potentially hiding bad loans (evergreening).
3.  **Capital Flight:** Startups and investors are fleeing to Dubai/Singapore.
4.  **The Result:** The financial system looks "Robust" on government reports, but it is built on **Unsecured Retail Loans** and **Conglomerate Exposure**. If consumption stops (Phase 4), the entire banking sector could face a "Systemic Shock."
