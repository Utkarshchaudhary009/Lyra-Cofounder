## 1. The 4-Year Contract Trap
*   **The Wait:** It takes **1,445 days** (almost 4 years) to enforce a simple business contract in India.
*   **The Global Rank:** India was ranked 163 out of 190 (before the report was stopped). This is slower than many small island nations.
*   **The Result:** Companies are scared to do business with new partners because if they get cheated, the court will take half a decade to give a verdict.

## 2. Land: The Expensive Battle
*   **The Cost:** In industrial zones like Haryana, land prices jumped to **₹5.8 Crore per acre** in 2024.
*   **The Delay:** Buying land for a factory takes **4 to 5 years** due to messy paperwork and local political protests.
*   **The Barrier:** High land costs act as a "Keep Out" sign for small manufacturers, leaving the land only for massive conglomerates (Phase 17).

## 3. Logistics: The Time Tax
*   **The Number:** India's official logistics cost is **7.97% of GDP**.
*   **The Reality:** While the cost looks okay, the efficiency is low. India ranks **38th** in the world. Trucks spend more time at state borders and in traffic (Phase 13) than they do driving. This makes "Just-in-Time" manufacturing impossible.

## 4. Electricity: The Industrial Tax
*   **The Burden:** Industrial users pay a **"Cross-Subsidy"**—higher bills to make power free for farmers (Phase 1).
*   **The Loss:** In some states, commercial users pay **36% more** than the actual cost of power. This makes "Made in India" products more expensive than "Made in Vietnam."

## 5. Corruption: The Bribery Rate
*   **The Rank:** India is **96th** in the Corruption Perception Index (2024).
*   **The Hidden Cost:** The bribery rate in India is **39%**—the highest in Asia. Bribes account for roughly **6% of all investment money**. This is a "Corruption Tax" on every new factory.

## 6. Single Window: A Half-Open Window
*   **The System:** The National Single Window (NSWS) was meant to simplify approvals.
*   **The Backlog:** There are still **2.29 lakh pending approvals**.
*   **The Feedback:** Users say it’s just a website that tells you which forms to file, but you still have to go to 20 different offices to actually get the work done.

## 7. Stalled Labor Laws
*   **The Progress:** New Labor Codes finally "started" in Nov 2025 after 5 years of delay.
*   **The Mess:** Only **3 out of 28 states** have actually implemented the rules. Because labor is a state subject, a company in West Bengal follows different rules than one in Gujarat, making national expansion a legal nightmare.

## 8. The ₹16 Lakh Crore Tax War
*   **The Backlog:** Over **5.3 lakh income tax cases** are stuck in courts.
*   **The Money:** **₹16.75 lakh crore** ($200 Billion) is locked in these disputes. That’s more than the entire education budget of India. The government and businesses are fighting forever, while the money sits useless.

## 9. Bankruptcy: Hard to Die
*   **The Exit:** The Bankruptcy Code (IBC) was meant to help companies close fast.
*   **The Delay:** It now takes **713 days** to close a failing company. 
*   **The Loss:** By the time a company is sold, its machines are rusted and its value is gone. Creditors only get back **32% of their money**.

## 10. Synthesis: Easy to Start, Hard to Survive
1.  **Red Tape:** 1,450 annual filings and 59 types of inspectors (Phase 16).
2.  **Slow Justice:** 4 years for a contract and 2 years for bankruptcy.
3.  **High Input Costs:** Expensive land and "Subsidy-Taxed" electricity.
4.  **The Result:** India is great at "Announcing" reforms but terrible at "Implementing" them. It is a country of **High Friction**, where an entrepreneur spends more time fighting the "System" than fighting the "Competition."
