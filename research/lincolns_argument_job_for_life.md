# "Is the Concept of a 'Job for Life' Officially Dead?"


### *Addressed to students and teachers — 3 Minute Speech*

---

"Good afternoon, respected teachers and friends. I am Utkarsh Chaudhary, proudly representing Kanchanjanga House."

(pause — look at the audience — then begin slowly)

"Sir, ma'am, friends — let me tell you about a man we all know."

He cleared a government exam in 1994. He got posted in a district office. He wore the same safari suit for twenty-seven years. He lived the same day, again and again, for three decades. And the entire mohalla called him *"settled."*

Then — last year — his department was digitised. Half the posts were declared redundant. He was offered VRS — Voluntary Retirement Scheme — which, sir, is just a polite way of saying, *"Please leave quietly."*

**I am sixteen years old. And I am being prepared for the same life that just collapsed under his feet.**

---

My mumma still says: *"Beta, UPSC karo. Life set ho jayegi."* Even after I am in Maths.

And I respect that. **I respect it deeply.** Because in her time, a permanent job was not just employment — it was **survival.**

**But her time is not my time.**

---

Walk into any coaching centre in Prayagraj. In Kota. In Mukherjee Nagar. Look at the faces.

You will see **nineteen-year-olds** who have been preparing for the same exam for four years. Five years. Some — even six years. They have no friends. They have no hobbies. They have no life — because they've been told that life *begins after selection.*

And the selection rate?

**0.01%.**

The "job for life" did not just promise security. **It consumed the youth of an entire generation as fuel — and gave most of them nothing in return.**

---

We — in this classroom — are being taught to pass exams. To memorise. To reproduce. We are being trained for a world that **no longer exists.**

The world outside this room runs on AI, on code, on creativity — on skills that no board syllabus in India even acknowledges. A boy in a village in Jaunpur is earning a lakh a month doing video editing on Fiverr. And we are memorising chapters that even the system has moved past.

Sir, you cannot prepare students for 2030 using a syllabus designed for 1990 and then blame them when they are *unemployable.*

**You cannot hand someone a map of a city that no longer exists and then call them lost.**

---

> Our parents were told: *"Get a degree."* They got a degree. The degree became worthless.  
> Our parents were told: *"Your turn will come."* It never came. But the EMIs did.

And now — the same system turns to **us** and says: *"Trust the process."*

Which process? **The one that failed them?**

---

We did not kill the "job for life."

**We are simply the first generation honest enough to stop pretending it is still alive.**

The "job for life" is dead.

**But life — real, full, self-built life — has never been more possible than it is right now, in this room, for every single one of us.**
